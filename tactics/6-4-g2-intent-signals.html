Tactic 6.4 - Signal-Based / Intent

G2/Review Site Intent Signals

Identify companies actively researching your category on G2, TrustRadius, or Capterra. Review site activity signals high purchase intent and active evaluation. Reach out when they are comparing options and most receptive to vendor conversations.

Effort: Low Cost: Medium-High ICP: Technical Buyers, Operations Teams
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: When someone from a target company visits your G2 page or category on review sites, they are actively researching solutions. This is bottom-of-funnel behaviour indicating imminent purchase decision. Your outreach arrives exactly when they are ready to talk to vendors.

The psychology behind review site intent

  • Active evaluation mode - People browsing review sites are not passively learning. They are comparing specific vendors to make purchase decision. High intent behaviour that indicates budget and authority
  • Pre-qualified interest - Visiting your G2 profile means they already know your category applies to their problem. You skip awareness stage and jump straight to consideration
  • Comparison mindset - Review site visitors are explicitly comparing vendors. Your outreach helps them make informed decision rather than interrupting with cold pitch
  • Trust through transparency - Referencing their review site research shows you respect their due diligence process. Positions you as helpful resource rather than pushy sales person

Why review site signals specifically

Unlike vague website visits, review site activity is unambiguous buying behaviour. No one casually browses G2 for fun. Every visit represents someone with problem actively looking for solution. This is highest-intent signal available at scale.

The compounding effect: Companies researching on review sites often involve multiple stakeholders. One visitor from a company likely means buying committee is forming. Early outreach positions you to influence entire evaluation process.

04

Best Practices

▼

Intent Signal Qualification - Filtering Noise

▼

Not all intent signals are equal

G2 visitor lists include everything from hot prospects to competitors doing research. Qualify signals before investing outreach time to focus on genuine buying opportunities.

Qualification criteria

  • Company ICP fit - Do they match your ideal customer profile by size, industry, geography? Non-ICP companies waste time even if they show intent
  • Engagement depth - Did they view one page or browse multiple pages and reviews? Deeper engagement signals stronger intent
  • Recency and frequency - Single visit 3 weeks ago is weak. Multiple visits this week is strong. Recent repeat visitors are hottest prospects
  • Competitive research filter - Check if visitor company is direct competitor or partner doing market research. Filter these out

Intent signal scoring

Signal TypeIntent ScoreAction Priority
Multiple team members visited in 7 days Very High Immediate outreach to senior stakeholder. Buying committee likely forming. Highest priority
Single visitor, multiple pages, recent High Outreach within 24 hours. Strong individual intent. Good opportunity
Single visitor, category page only Medium Nurture sequence. Early stage research. Worth engaging but lower urgency
Visit over 30 days ago Low Add to general nurture. Window likely closed. Low conversion probability
Quality filter: Only reach out to companies where you can articulate clear value proposition for their specific context. Intent signal without relevant positioning wastes the opportunity.

Research and Context Building - Know Who Visited

▼

Building visitor profile

G2 intent data shows company name but not individual visitor identity in most cases. Use LinkedIn and other sources to identify likely visitor and build context for personalised outreach.

Identifying the visitor

  • Search LinkedIn by company and role - Look for roles that typically evaluate your category. For sales tools, search for Sales Ops, RevOps, VP Sales at company
  • Check recent activity - Recent LinkedIn posts or profile updates mentioning tool evaluation or pain points in your category signal likely researcher
  • Multiple potential matches - If several people could be the visitor, reach out to most senior relevant person. They likely have final authority
  • New hires - People in role 0-3 months often tasked with evaluating tools. Check for recent hires in relevant functions

Outreach Messaging - Helpful Not Pushy

▼

Positioning as research assistant

Your outreach should help them make better evaluation decision, not pressure them to choose you. Position as insider resource who can accelerate their research and provide unbiased guidance.

Message framework for intent-based outreach

  • Acknowledge research behaviour - "Noticed someone from Acme was checking out our G2 profile" establishes context without being creepy
  • Offer to help evaluation - "Happy to answer any questions that came up during your research" positions you as helpful resource
  • Provide decision-making value - Share comparison guide, decision framework, or buyer guide for category. Help them evaluate all options not just yours
  • Low-friction next step - "Quick call to discuss what you are looking for?" not "Book a demo." Match their research stage

Timing and Speed - Striking While Fresh

▼

The 24-hour window

Intent signals are time-sensitive. Prospect researched your solution today. Your brand is fresh in their mind right now. Waiting 3-5 days means they have moved on to other vendors or decision fatigue has set in.

Optimal response timeline

  • Same day if possible - Best outcome. They literally just looked at your profile hours ago. Message lands while evaluation is active in their mind
  • Within 24 hours - Still strong. Brand recall is high. They likely have not made decision yet. Good window for engagement
  • 24-48 hours - Acceptable but declining. They may have already started conversations with other vendors. Still worth reaching out
  • 48+ hours - Poor timing. Window likely closed. They have moved to next stage of evaluation or forgotten about initial research. Low conversion

Setting up speed workflow

05

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Leading with aggressive sales pitch

They just researched you. Immediate "book a demo" push feels pushy and ignores their research stage. They disengage.
Position as helpful resource for their evaluation. Offer to answer questions, provide comparison guidance, or share relevant content. Help them decide, do not pressure.

2. Slow follow-up on intent signals

Waiting 4-5 days to reach out means they have moved on to other vendors or lost momentum. Your brand is no longer fresh. Window closed.
Set up daily intent review workflow. Reach out same day or within 24 hours maximum. Speed is competitive advantage for intent-based outreach.

3. No qualification of intent signals

Reaching out to every company that visits G2 includes competitors, non-ICP, and casual researchers. Waste time on unqualified opportunities.
Filter intent signals against ICP criteria before outreach. Only contact companies that match ideal customer profile and show genuine buying behaviour.

4. Generic messaging that ignores research context

Sending standard cold email template to intent signal wastes the context advantage. They know you should know they researched you.
Explicitly reference their G2 visit in message. Tailor value proposition based on which pages they viewed and depth of engagement. Make it obviously personalised.

5. Reaching out to wrong person

Intent data shows company not individual. Guessing wrong decision maker and reaching out to irrelevant person wastes opportunity.
Research likely visitor based on role and recent activity. If uncertain, reach out to most senior relevant person. They can redirect if needed.

6. Not investing in intent data product

Trying to manually track G2 visitors or guessing who researched you is impossible at scale. Miss most opportunities.
Budget for G2 Buyer Intent or TrustRadius Intent product. Expensive but ROI is strong. Highest-intent leads available. Prioritise over lower-intent channels.
