Tactic 10.2 - AI-Powered

AI-Generated Personalised Video

Use AI avatars to create personalised video messages at scale. Record one template video, AI generates thousands of variations with each prospect's name, company, and custom talking points. Video personalisation without recording burden.

Effort: Low Cost: Medium ICP: All Buyer Personas
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Video outreach dramatically outperforms text-based outreach but traditionally does not scale. AI-generated video solves this by combining video's emotional impact and personalisation with automation's scale. You get both effectiveness and efficiency.

The psychology behind AI video effectiveness

  • Visual novelty and attention - Video in inbox stands out from text emails. Thumbnail of person speaking captures attention and drives curiosity to click
  • Perceived effort and reciprocity - Even AI-generated video feels like more effort than text email. Recipients feel obligation to watch and respond to apparent personalised effort
  • Emotional connection through face - Seeing face and hearing voice creates stronger connection than text. Mirror neurons activate. Feels more human and trustworthy
  • Information retention advantage - Video combines visual and auditory channels. People retain information better from video than text. Your message sticks longer

Why AI-generated video specifically

Recording personal video for each prospect is impossible at scale. Generic batch video lacks personalisation punch. AI-generated video delivers true personalisation at infinite scale by synthesising custom videos from single template recording.

The compounding effect: AI video quality improves rapidly. Early versions looked uncanny. Current generation is nearly indistinguishable from real recordings. As quality improves, effectiveness increases while cost decreases.

Best Practices

▼

Template Recording Quality - Foundation Matters

▼

Template quality determines all output quality

AI can only work with what you give it. Poor lighting, bad audio, or awkward delivery in template means poor quality in every generated video. Invest in template recording quality upfront.

Technical recording requirements

  • Lighting setup - Face evenly lit from front. Avoid harsh shadows or backlighting. Natural daylight or ring light works well. Dark or unevenly lit footage produces poor clones
  • Audio quality - Use external microphone not laptop mic. Clear voice without echo or background noise. AI voice cloning requires clean audio input
  • Camera position - Eye level, centred framing. Look directly at camera not slightly off. Stable footage not handheld. Professional setup improves clone quality
  • Background - Simple neutral background. Avoid busy patterns or movement. Plain wall or blurred background works best. Busy backgrounds distract from message

Delivery and performance tips

ElementBest PracticeWhy It Matters
Energy and enthusiasm Slightly elevated energy, warm smile, engaged tone Video amplifies low energy. Need to over-deliver enthusiasm for it to feel natural on camera
Speaking pace Moderate pace with clear enunciation. Not rushed AI needs clear pronunciation to clone accurately. Fast mumbling produces poor synthesis
Natural pauses Brief pauses between thoughts. Not continuous stream Pauses make AI-generated speech sound more natural. Helps with variable insertion
Scripted versus conversational Memorise key points, speak conversationally not reading Reading script looks stiff. Conversational delivery feels authentic even when AI-cloned
Multiple takes strategy: Record template 5-10 times. Pick best take for AI training. This is foundation for thousands of videos. Worth extra 30 minutes to get perfect source material.

Personalisation Variable Strategy - Beyond Name

▼

Name personalisation is table stakes

Simply saying prospect name is not enough differentiation. Everyone uses AI video now. Need deeper personalisation variables that demonstrate genuine research and relevance.

Personalisation variable types

  • Basic identifiers - Name, company, role. Foundation but not sufficient alone. Every AI video uses these
  • Contextual hooks - Recent company news, funding, product launch, job posting. Shows you did research. More impactful than generic variables
  • Pain point references - Specific challenge relevant to their role or industry. "Companies in fintech struggling with X." Demonstrates category understanding
  • Mutual connections or customers - "Saw you know James at Acme, one of our customers." Social proof plus personalisation compound

Variable depth by tier

TierVariables UsedResearch TimeImpact
Tier 1 - High-value accounts Name, company, recent news, specific pain point, mutual connection 10-15 min research per prospect Very high. Feels truly custom. Worth investment for strategic accounts
Tier 2 - Mid-market targets Name, company, industry-specific challenge, recent funding or hire 5 min research per prospect High. Good personalisation without excessive research burden
Tier 3 - Volume outreach Name, company, generic industry hook 1 min lookup per prospect Medium. Better than no personalisation. Acceptable at scale
Variable sourcing automation: Use Clay or similar enrichment tools to automatically populate many personalisation variables. Pull recent news, funding, job changes from APIs rather than manual research.

Delivery and Distribution - Making Videos Easy to Watch

▼

Video must be frictionless to consume

Requiring download or multiple clicks to watch kills engagement. Make video as easy as possible to play. Inline playback in email or single-click landing page maximises view rates.

Distribution channel options

  • Email with inline GIF thumbnail - Animated GIF shows first few seconds of video. Clicking plays full video on landing page. Works in all email clients. Highest engagement
  • Video landing page link - Email contains link to dedicated landing page with video player. Allows tracking and calls-to-action around video. More control but extra click
  • LinkedIn video message - Send video directly in LinkedIn message. Native playback. Good for LinkedIn-heavy prospects but limited tracking
  • Embedded in email sequence - Video as touchpoint 2 or 3 in automated sequence. Escalates from text to video for non-responders. Good for warming up cold prospects

Landing page optimisation

ElementBest PracticePurpose
Video player Auto-play on page load with sound muted. Large clear player Immediate engagement. No click required. Viewer controls sound
Call-to-action Clear CTA below video. Calendar link or reply button Give immediate next step while video message is fresh. Reduce friction to response
Additional context Brief text summary of video content. Links to relevant resources Supports different learning styles. Provides takeaway even if they skim video
Mobile optimisation Responsive player that works on all devices Many prospects watch on mobile. Poor mobile experience kills conversions
Thumbnail importance: Custom thumbnail showing your face mid-smile with prospect company name overlay dramatically increases play rates. Generic play button thumbnail underperforms.

Transparency and Ethics - Disclosing AI Generation

▼

Deception versus enhancement debate

AI video quality is now good enough that many recipients cannot tell it is synthetic. This raises ethical question: should you disclose AI generation or let it pass as real recording?

Disclosure approaches

  • No disclosure - Send AI video as if it were real recording. Maximises perception of effort. Risk of backlash if recipient discovers deception
  • Subtle disclosure - Small text on landing page: "Video personalised using AI." Transparent but not prominently advertised. Balances honesty and effectiveness
  • Prominent disclosure - Open about AI in email: "Created this AI video just for you." Positions as cool technology not deception. Some find it impressive
  • Hybrid approach - Use AI for personalisation elements only. Record generic opening yourself, AI fills in name and company. Best of both worlds

Factors influencing disclosure decision

FactorDisclosure RecommendedRationale
Audience tech sophistication High if technical audience Technical buyers may spot AI and feel deceived if not disclosed. Better to be upfront
Brand positioning High if innovation-focused brand AI disclosure reinforces innovative positioning. Aligns with brand values
Regulatory environment High if regulated industry Some industries may have disclosure requirements. Better safe than sorry
Relationship stage High for existing relationships People you already know will recognise AI. Disclosure prevents awkwardness
Recommendation: Subtle disclosure balances ethics and effectiveness. Small text on landing page "Personalised using AI technology" provides transparency without undermining impact. Test both approaches and let data guide decision.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Poor quality template recording

Bad lighting, poor audio, or low-energy delivery in template produces thousands of poor-quality videos. First impression damage at scale.
Invest in proper recording setup. Good lighting, external mic, stable camera. Record multiple takes and pick best one. Template quality is foundation.

2. Only personalising with name

Just saying prospect name is not enough differentiation. Every AI video tool does this. Feels generic despite being "personalised."
Include deeper variables like recent company news, role-specific pain points, or mutual connections. Demonstrate actual research beyond name lookup.

3. Videos too long

2-3 minute AI videos lose attention. Viewer drops off halfway through. Message diluted by rambling.
Keep videos 30-60 seconds maximum. One clear message and call-to-action. Shorter videos have higher completion rates and better retention.

4. Difficult viewing experience

Requiring download or multiple clicks to watch creates friction. Many prospects give up before watching.
Use inline GIF thumbnail in email that plays on single click. Or auto-play landing page. Make viewing frictionless. Every extra step loses viewers.

5. No tracking or follow-up strategy

Sending video without tracking who watched means missing engagement signals. High-intent viewers do not get prioritised.
Use platform tracking to see who watched and for how long. Follow up quickly with engaged viewers. Treat 100% view as hot lead signal.

6. Uncanny valley effect from poor clone

Cheap AI video platforms produce slightly off clones that feel creepy. Uncanny valley effect repels prospects.
Invest in quality platform like HeyGen or Synthesia. Test clone quality before sending at scale. Slightly robotic is worse than obviously synthetic.
