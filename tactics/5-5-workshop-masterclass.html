Tactic 5.5 - Event-Based Plays

Workshop/Masterclass Series

Run a free multi-session workshop or masterclass series teaching valuable skills related to your product category. Attract ideal buyers, demonstrate expertise, and nurture attendees into qualified pipeline over 3-4 weeks of consistent engagement.

Effort: High Cost: Low-Medium ICP: Founders, Sales Leaders, Ops Professionals
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Decision makers are drowning in product pitches but starving for practical skills training. A multi-session workshop creates sustained engagement over weeks, builds genuine trust through education, and naturally segments participants by engagement level into qualified pipeline.

The psychology behind workshop series

  • Commitment and consistency - When someone attends session one, they feel invested. Psychological commitment drives attendance at sessions two, three, and four. Each session deepens the relationship
  • Authority positioning - Teaching a skill positions you as the expert authority in the category. Attendees begin to associate your expertise with the solution category itself
  • Gradual trust building - A single webinar can educate but rarely builds deep trust. Four sessions over four weeks creates repeated positive interactions that compound into genuine relationship
  • Natural qualification - Engagement signals across multiple sessions reveal who is genuinely interested versus tire kickers. High attendance equals high intent

Why workshop format specifically

Unlike single webinars that feel transactional, a workshop series creates a learning journey. Participants expect to put in effort, complete exercises between sessions, and engage with the content deeply. This active participation creates far stronger emotional investment than passive consumption.

The compounding effect: Each session builds on the previous one. Attendees who complete the full series have invested 4-6 hours with you. That level of time investment creates tremendous switching cost and naturally leads to sales conversations.

04

Best Practices

▼

Curriculum Design - Teaching Adjacent Skills

▼

Adjacent skill selection

The workshop topic should teach a valuable skill that your product supports but is not purely about your product. Teach the process, methodology, or framework first. The product becomes the accelerator of what they have learned.

Session arc structure

  • Session 1 - Foundations and Frameworks - Establish the why behind the skill. Share frameworks and mental models. Minimal product mention. Pure value delivery
  • Session 2 - Tactical Deep Dive - Get into specific tactics and step-by-step processes. Share templates and checklists. Include exercise attendees complete before next session
  • Session 3 - Implementation and Scaling - Address common obstacles and how to overcome them. Introduce how tools and technology can accelerate the process. Natural place for product introduction
  • Session 4 - Advanced Optimisation - Share advanced tactics for people who have mastered basics. Optional product demo showing how to implement workshop concepts at scale

Value balance over sessions

SessionEducational ContentProduct MentionGoal
Session 1 90% 10% Establish credibility and deliver massive value upfront to ensure return attendance
Session 2 85% 15% Continue education while beginning to introduce product as enabler of concepts being taught
Session 3 70% 30% Show product in action as natural extension of methodology. Make clear how it solves problems discussed
Session 4 60% 40% Transition qualified high-intent participants toward sales conversations. Offer exclusive deal for attendees
Content reuse strategy: Each session should produce 3-5 standalone pieces of content such as frameworks, templates, checklists. These become social posts, blog articles, and lead magnets you promote continuously to drive registrations for next cohort.

Promotion and Registration - Filling Seats

▼

Promotion timeline

Begin promoting 3-4 weeks before first session. Create urgency with early bird bonuses for first 50 registrants. Continue promoting up until day before session one to maximise registrations.

Multi-channel promotion approach

  • Email existing list - Send dedicated emails to existing contacts and customers. Segment by role and personalise messaging around how workshop applies to their function
  • LinkedIn organic - Share session previews, behind-the-scenes curriculum development, and testimonials from previous participants if you have run workshops before
  • LinkedIn ads - Target specific job titles in your ICP. Promote the value of skills being taught, not just the workshop itself. Budget £500-£1000 for 4-week promotion window
  • Partner co-promotion - Collaborate with complementary tools or consultants who serve same audience. They promote to their list, you promote to yours. Expands reach significantly

Delivery and Engagement - Keeping Attendance High

▼

Combating drop-off across sessions

Expect 40-50% drop-off from session one to final session. This is normal. The key is maximising engagement of those who do attend all sessions, as they become your highest-intent prospects.

Engagement tactics during live sessions

  • Interactive exercises - Every 15-20 minutes, pause for attendee exercise or discussion. Breakout rooms, polls, or chat responses keep energy high
  • Real-time Q&A - Reserve final 10-15 minutes for questions. Address specific attendee situations. This personalisation makes content feel custom to them
  • Accountability mechanisms - Assign homework between sessions. Ask attendees to share results in next session. Public commitment increases completion rates
  • Community building - Create Slack channel or LinkedIn group for series participants. Encourage questions and discussion between sessions. Peer support reduces drop-off

Follow-up cadence between sessions

Conversion and Follow-Up - Turning Attendees into Customers

▼

Engagement scoring and segmentation

Not all attendees are equal prospects. Segment based on engagement signals to prioritise follow-up efforts on highest-intent participants.

Attendee segments and follow-up approach

  • High intent - Attended 3-4 sessions, asked questions, completed exercises. These are hot leads. Immediate personalised outreach offering free consultation to implement workshop concepts
  • Medium intent - Attended 2-3 sessions or watched recordings. Warm leads. Email sequence highlighting how product accelerates what they learned. Invite to product demo
  • Low intent - Registered but attended 0-1 sessions. Cool leads. Nurture with recordings and content. May convert in future cohorts
  • Champions - Highly engaged and vocal. Ask for testimonials and case studies. These become promotion assets for future workshop cohorts

Conversion offers for workshop graduates

Offer TypeAppealConversion Rate Boost
Exclusive workshop pricing Special discount only for series participants Rewards their time investment. Creates urgency with limited window after final session
Free implementation consultation 30-60 min working session applying workshop concepts to their business Low barrier to entry. Natural transition from education to sales conversation
Extended trial with setup support Longer trial period plus guided setup based on workshop frameworks Removes friction of figuring out setup alone. Increases activation and adoption
Long-term nurture: Even attendees who do not convert immediately are far warmer than cold prospects. Add them to specialised nurture sequence with advanced content. They may convert 3-6 months later when timing is right.
05

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Making it too product-focused too early

Leading with product pitch in first session breaks trust. Attendees registered for education, not a sales pitch. They disengage immediately.
Keep first two sessions 85-90% pure education. Introduce product gradually in sessions 3-4 as natural extension of concepts taught. Let value build trust first.

2. Sessions too long and lecture-heavy

90-minute lecture with no interaction loses attention. Attendees multitask, disengage, and drop off for future sessions.
Keep sessions to 45-60 minutes maximum. Include interactive elements every 15-20 minutes such as polls, exercises, breakout discussions. Engagement sustains attention.

3. No homework or exercises between sessions

Without application between sessions, content does not stick. Attendees forget what was covered and lose momentum.
Assign simple homework or provide template to implement before next session. Creates accountability and keeps workshop top of mind throughout the week.

4. Poor follow-up after final session

Delivering great workshop then going silent wastes all the relationship-building. Attendees move on and forget about you.
Have clear conversion offer ready for final session. Follow up within 24 hours with personalised outreach to high-engagement attendees. Strike while momentum is high.

5. Not recording sessions

People who miss live sessions drop out entirely. You lose potential conversions. You also miss chance to create evergreen content asset.
Record every session and send to all registrants within 2 hours. Let people catch up asynchronously. Repurpose recordings as standalone lead magnets.

6. Treating all attendees the same in follow-up

Someone who attended all 4 sessions and asked questions is far hotter lead than someone who registered but never showed up. Generic follow-up wastes time.
Segment by engagement level. Immediate personalised outreach to high-engagement folks. Automated nurture for low-engagement. Prioritise your time on warmest leads.
