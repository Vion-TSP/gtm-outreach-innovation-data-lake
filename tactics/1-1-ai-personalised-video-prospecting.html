AI-Personalised Video Prospecting

01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Video humanises cold outreach instantly. Seeing a face and hearing a voice builds trust faster than any text-based message. AI-powered personalisation tools now enable you to send 500 personalised videos with the same effort it used to take to record 5.

The psychology behind video prospecting

  • Pattern interruption - Video stands out in a sea of text emails. Most decision-makers receive zero video emails per week
  • Trust acceleration - Face-to-face communication builds rapport 10x faster than written messages. Video approximates this effect at scale
  • Cognitive ease - Watching a 60-second video requires less cognitive effort than reading 3 emails. Lower friction means higher engagement
  • Proof of effort - Even AI-generated videos signal you invested more effort than a templated email. Effort = respect = response

Why AI-personalisation matters

Without AI, personalised video doesn't scale. Recording 100 individual videos takes 8-10 hours. With AI tools, you record one template, upload a CSV of prospect data, and generate 100 personalised videos in 20 minutes. The AI dynamically inserts names, company logos, website screenshots, and even custom value propositions per prospect.

The authenticity question: Does AI-generated video feel fake? Early tools did. Modern lip-sync and voice cloning tools (HeyGen, Tavus, Synthesia) are now indistinguishable from real video for 60-90 second clips. The key: use them for scale, but reserve fully manual video for your top 50 accounts.

Video Structure (Under 60 Seconds)

▼
SectionDurationContentTips
Intro 5 sec Your face + their name. "Hey Sarah, quick video for you." Say their name immediately. Smile. Make eye contact with camera
Hook 15 sec Something specific about their company. Screen-share their website or LinkedIn profile Mention a real detail: recent funding, job posting, blog post, product feature. Proves you did homework
Value 25 sec One key insight or what you'd do for them. Be specific, not generic. Reference similar company results Don't pitch your product. Pitch an insight or outcome. "Companies like Datadog use this approach to solve X problem"
CTA 10 sec "Would love 15 min to walk you through how we did this for Ramp. Calendar link below." Low-friction CTA. 15 min, not 30. Offer specific time slots if possible

Total video length: 50-60 seconds. Do not exceed 90 seconds. Completion rates drop off a cliff after 60s.

Script tip: Write your script first. Read it aloud. Time it. If over 60s, cut everything that isn't essential. Shorter = higher completion rate.

Thumbnails (The Make-or-Break Element)

▼

Why thumbnails matter more than the video itself

A perfect video with a generic thumbnail gets 10-15% play rate. An average video with a personalised thumbnail gets 50-60% play rate. The thumbnail is the gatekeeper. If they don't click play, the video quality is irrelevant.

High-converting thumbnail tactics

  • Whiteboard with their name - Hold a whiteboard or notepad with their first name written in marker. Play rate: 50-70%. Time: 30 sec per thumbnail
  • Face visible + custom text overlay - Your face in the thumbnail with text overlay: "Hey Sarah - 60 sec idea for Acme". Play rate: 40-55%. Time: 2 min per thumbnail (Canva batch creation)
  • Screenshot of their website with annotation - Screen capture their website with a red circle or arrow pointing to something specific. Face picture-in-picture. Play rate: 35-50%. Time: 3 min per thumbnail
  • Their company logo + your face - Split screen: their logo on left, your face on right. Play rate: 30-45%. Time: 1 min per thumbnail (template reuse)

What NOT to do

  • Generic thumbnail with just your face and no personalisation - Play rate: 10-15%
  • Text-heavy thumbnail with 3+ lines of copy - Unreadable on mobile. Play rate drops 20%
  • No face visible (just a logo or screen recording) - Lacks human connection. Play rate: 8-12%
  • Using the auto-generated thumbnail from video platform - Almost never optimal. Always custom create
Time investment: Custom thumbnails take 1-3 min each. For 100 videos, that's 2-3 hours of work. Worth it. The thumbnail drives a 3-4x improvement in engagement, which translates directly to meetings booked.

▼

200+ mid-market prospects (Directors, VPs, Managers) AI-generated (HeyGen, Tavus) Scale wins. Manually recording 200 videos takes 15+ hours. AI does it in 30 min

Hybrid approach: Use AI for volume (200+ prospects/month), but manually record videos for anyone who watches 50%+ of the AI video. That engagement signal justifies the manual effort.

Embedding Videos in Email

▼

Why embedding matters

Never send a raw video link. Clickthrough rates for raw links are 5-10%. A thumbnail image embedded in the email with a play button overlay gets 35-50% clickthrough. The visual cue (thumbnail + play button) signals "this is a video" and triggers curiosity.

How to embed

  1. Upload video to hosting platform (Loom, Vidyard, HeyGen, etc.)
  2. Generate a custom thumbnail (see Thumbnails section above)
  3. Use the platform's "embed in email" feature to generate HTML with thumbnail + play button overlay
  4. Insert HTML into email body (most platforms integrate with Gmail, Outreach, Salesloft, HubSpot)
  5. When recipient clicks thumbnail, they're taken to hosted video landing page (not played inline in email - most email clients block inline video)

Email copy structure

The video doesn't replace the email copy. The copy drives them to watch the video.

ElementExample
Subject line "Sarah - 60 sec idea for Acme" or "quick video for you, Sarah"
Opening line "Made a quick video walking through an idea for Acme. 60 seconds:"
Video thumbnail (Embedded thumbnail image with play button)
Fallback link "Can't see the video? Watch it here: link"
Close "Worth a quick chat? Calendar link below."
Pro tip: Send the video email between 8-10 AM in their timezone. Video emails sent in the morning get 40% higher view rates than afternoon sends. People have more attention bandwidth early in the day.
05

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Video too long (over 90 seconds)

Completion rate drops from 60% at 60s to 25% at 2 min. Long videos signal you don't value their time.
Cap at 60 seconds. Script it. Time it. Cut ruthlessly. If you can't say it in 60s, it's not clear enough.

2. Generic script with no personalisation

"Hi, I'm James from Acme and we help companies like yours..." sounds like every other cold video. No hook.
Lead with something specific about them: "Saw you're hiring 2 AEs" or "Noticed your Series B announcement". Personalisation in the first 10 seconds or they stop watching.

3. Bad lighting or poor audio quality

Looks unprofessional. Signals low-quality product. Kills credibility instantly.
Natural light facing you (not behind). Use a ring light if indoors. Invest in a $50 USB microphone. Audio quality matters more than video quality.

4. No clear CTA at the end

They watch the video, think "interesting", and then forget about it. No next step = no action.
End with one specific CTA: "Calendar link below to grab 15 min" or "Reply with a good time this week". One CTA only. Make it frictionless.

5. Sending video without tracking

You don't know who watched, how much they watched, or when. Can't prioritise follow-ups.
Use video tools with built-in analytics (Loom, Vidyard, HeyGen all have this). Set up webhook alerts for 50%+ view completion. Follow up within 24h on viewers.

6. No follow-up on video viewers

Someone watched 80% of your video and you never followed up. That's a warm lead you ignored.
Create a daily task: check video analytics, pull list of 50%+ viewers, call or email within 24h: "Saw you watched the video - worth a quick chat?"

7. Using AI when manual is better

Sending an AI-generated video to your top 10 dream accounts signals laziness, not scale.
Manual video for top 50 accounts, AI for volume. When in doubt, go manual. The ROI on one enterprise deal justifies 10 hours of manual video recording.

8. Generic thumbnail (or no custom thumbnail)

Generic thumbnails get 10-15% play rate. You spent 30 min on the video and 0 min on the thumbnail. Backwards.
Spend 2-3 min per custom thumbnail. Whiteboard with their name, or custom text overlay. The thumbnail drives the play rate, not the video quality.
