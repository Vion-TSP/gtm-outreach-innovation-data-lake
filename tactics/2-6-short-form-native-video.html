Tactic 2.6 - LinkedIn-Native

Short-Form Native Video (Under 30s)

Sub-30 second vertical videos posted natively on LinkedIn. High completion and interaction rates. No external hosting or professional editing required. Phone-shot authenticity wins.

01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: LinkedIn's algorithm heavily favours native video over external links. Short-form vertical video optimised for mobile gets massive organic reach. Under 30 seconds drives high completion rates, which feeds the algorithm. Authenticity trumps production value.

Why LinkedIn prioritises short native video

  • Platform retention - Native video keeps users on LinkedIn. External links take them away. Algorithm rewards native content with 3-5x more reach
  • Mobile-first consumption - 70% of LinkedIn usage is mobile. Vertical video fills the screen, creating an immersive experience. Horizontal video wastes screen real estate
  • Completion rate signals quality - LinkedIn's algorithm tracks video completion. Videos under 30s get 60-80% completion. Videos over 60s drop to 20-30%. High completion = algorithm boost
  • Interaction velocity - Short videos get watched faster, driving quick engagement (likes, comments, shares). Fast interaction within the first hour signals virality to the algorithm

The psychology of short-form video

Attention spans on professional platforms are shrinking. Decision-makers won't commit to a 5-minute video from someone they don't know. But 20 seconds? Low risk. If it's good, they watch twice. If it's great, they share it. Short-form video is the new cold call - quick, direct, human.

Authenticity advantage: Phone-shot videos with imperfect lighting and casual delivery outperform studio-quality content. Why? They feel real. Professional production screams advertising. Raw phone videos signal genuine expertise and approachability.

04

Best Practices

▼

Format (Technical Requirements)

▼

Vertical 9:16 aspect ratio

Hold your phone vertically when recording. This is non-negotiable. Horizontal video on mobile wastes 60% of the screen space with black bars. Vertical video fills the entire mobile screen, creating an immersive viewing experience.

Duration: Under 30 seconds

Aim for 20-25 seconds. Maximum 30 seconds. Beyond 30s, completion rates drop sharply. Under 30s videos are also more likely to be watched multiple times, which boosts engagement signals.

Captions mandatory

80% of LinkedIn users watch videos on mute (during commutes, in open offices, in bed). If your video doesn't have captions, 80% of viewers won't understand it. Use auto-captioning tools (CapCut, Descript) - accuracy is 95%+ and takes 30 seconds to generate.

File format and quality

LinkedIn accepts MP4, MOV, and AVI. Modern smartphones shoot in 1080p, which is perfect. Don't overthink resolution. Clean audio matters more than 4K video. If recording indoors, use a quiet room to avoid echo or background noise.

Technical checklist: Vertical orientation, under 30s, captions visible, clear audio, 1080p minimum. Everything else is secondary.

Content Types (What to Talk About)

▼

Hot takes

Contrarian opinion on a common industry practice. Example: "Stop asking prospects for their budget. Here's why." Hot takes spark debate, which drives comments and shares. Controversy boosts reach.

Quick tips

One specific, actionable tactic that viewers can use immediately. Example: "Here's how to write a subject line that gets opened." Actionable content gets saved and reshared.

Behind-the-scenes

Show your actual work process. Example: "Here's how I research accounts before cold calling." Transparency builds trust and authority. People want to see how experts actually work.

Myth-busting

Call out a widespread misconception. Example: "Everyone says cold email is dead. Here's why they're wrong." Myth-busting positions you as an authority and corrects misinformation in your space.

Content source: Pull from client conversations, internal Slack debates, questions prospects ask repeatedly, or contrarian takes on industry best practices. If you've explained it twice this week, turn it into a video.

Hook (First 3 Seconds)

▼

The make-or-break moment

If you don't hook them in the first 3 seconds, they scroll. LinkedIn users are scanning their feed at high speed. Your opening line must stop the scroll. No fluff. No introductions. Lead with the insight.

Hook formats that work

  • Question: "Want to know why your outreach emails get ignored?" (Engages curiosity)
  • Bold statement: "Cold calling is more effective than cold email in 2026." (Sparks debate)
  • Pattern interrupt: "I used to think LinkedIn DMs were spam. I was wrong." (Challenges assumptions)
  • Specific claim: "This one subject line change doubled our open rate." (Promises value)

What NOT to do

  • Starting with "Hey everyone" or "In this video" - Wastes precious seconds
  • Introducing yourself first - They don't care who you are yet. Lead with value
  • Generic opening like "Here's a quick tip" - Not specific enough to grab attention
Test your hook: Show the first 3 seconds to a colleague with no context. If they don't immediately understand what the video is about, rewrite the hook.

Posting Time (When to Publish)

▼

Best days: Tuesday - Thursday

LinkedIn engagement peaks mid-week. Monday is inbox catch-up. Friday is mentally checked out. Tuesday through Thursday get the highest engagement and video completion rates.

Best times: 8-10 AM local time

Post when your target audience is commuting or starting their workday. Morning posts get higher engagement than afternoon. People have more attention bandwidth early in the day. Lunch (12-1 PM) is secondary peak.

Avoid posting after 5 PM

Evening posts get lower engagement unless your audience is in a different timezone. Professional content performs best during work hours. Save evening slots for personal or community-building content.

Timezone tip: If your ICP is in a different timezone, schedule posts for their 8-10 AM. LinkedIn's scheduler lets you set this up in advance.

Authenticity (Phone Recording > Professional Studio)

▼

Why imperfect wins

Professional studio videos with perfect lighting, backdrop, and editing feel like advertisements. Phone-shot videos with natural lighting and casual backgrounds feel like a colleague sharing an insight. On LinkedIn, authenticity beats polish.

What authenticity looks like

  • Natural lighting from a window (not ring lights)
  • Casual background (your office, kitchen, outside) - not a branded backdrop
  • Conversational tone - like you're talking to a friend, not presenting to a boardroom
  • Minimal editing - one take is fine. Don't over-polish. Small stumbles are humanising

When to use professional production

Save high-production video for product demos, case studies, or evergreen content that will live on your website. For LinkedIn short-form, authentic beats polished every time.

Authenticity test: If someone said "This looks like an ad", you've over-produced it. If they said "This feels like James just explained something to me", you nailed it.

Caption Style (Text Overlays)

▼

Big, bold, high-contrast text

Captions must be readable on a small mobile screen in a bright room. Use large font sizes. Bold weight. High contrast (white text on dark background or vice versa). Avoid thin fonts or low-contrast colours.

Positioning: Lower third

Place captions in the lower third of the video. This keeps them visible without covering your face. If you're showing a screen share, position captions above or below the key content area.

Timing: Word-by-word or short phrases

Captions should appear word-by-word or in short 2-3 word phrases. This keeps viewers engaged and makes the text easy to follow. Avoid showing full sentences at once - it's harder to read.

Style preference: Clean and simple

Don't over-style captions with flashy animations or emoji overload. Clean, bold text is most readable. Save creativity for your content, not your caption styling.

Caption test: Watch your video on your phone with brightness at 50%. If you can't easily read the captions, increase font size or contrast.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Video too long (over 60 seconds)

Completion rate drops sharply after 30s. Videos over 60s rarely get watched to the end. Low completion hurts algorithm ranking.
Keep it under 30s. If your point needs 60s, split it into two separate videos. Shorter always wins on LinkedIn.

2. No captions (or tiny unreadable captions)

80% of viewers watch on mute. No captions means 80% of viewers don't understand your content. Tiny captions are as bad as no captions.
Use auto-captioning tools. Large bold text, high contrast. Test on your phone before posting. If you can't read it easily, increase size.

3. Horizontal format instead of vertical

Horizontal video on mobile shows black bars above and below. Wastes 60% of screen space. Feels like old-school desktop video.
Always record vertically on your phone. 9:16 aspect ratio. Fill the mobile screen. Vertical video gets higher completion rates.

4. Over-produced (feels like an ad)

Perfect lighting, branded backdrops, and polished editing make it feel like a commercial. People skip ads. They watch authentic content.
Use natural lighting. Casual background. Conversational tone. One take is fine. Embrace minor imperfections. Authenticity beats perfection.

5. No hook in first 3 seconds

If you don't grab attention immediately, they scroll. Starting with "Hey everyone" or "In this video" wastes critical seconds.
Lead with the insight, question, or bold statement. No preamble. First 3 seconds should make them want to keep watching.

6. Posting inconsistently

One viral video doesn't build authority. Consistent presence does. Posting once a month means people forget you exist.
Aim for 2-3 short videos per week. Batch record on Monday, schedule through the week. Consistency beats perfection.
