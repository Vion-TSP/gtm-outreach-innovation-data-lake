Tactic 6.2 - Signal-Based / Intent

Funding Round Triggers

Target companies immediately after they announce funding rounds. Post-funding companies enter buying mode with budget to deploy, growth mandates to execute, and urgency to show momentum. Perfect timing for category-creating solutions.

Effort: Low Cost: Low-Medium ICP: Founders, C-suite Executives
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Companies raise funding to execute specific growth plans. In the 90 days post-funding, they are actively building infrastructure, hiring teams, and buying tools to deploy that capital. Budget objections evaporate when there is fresh cash in the bank and investor pressure to move fast.

The psychology behind funding triggers

  • Budget abundance mindset - Fresh capital removes price sensitivity temporarily. Founders shift from conservation mode to investment mode. They are looking for ways to spend strategically
  • Urgency to show progress - Investors expect to see momentum quickly. Companies feel pressure to deploy capital and hit milestones. This creates urgency that benefits vendors
  • Permission to upgrade infrastructure - Pre-funding they made do with free tools and scrappy solutions. Post-funding they have mandate to professionalise and invest in proper tooling
  • Growth mandate execution - Funding announcements often specify use of funds such as "expanding sales team" or "scaling operations." These stated priorities reveal exactly what they will buy

Why funding signals specifically

Unlike vague intent signals, funding announcements are public declarations of strategic direction backed by millions in capital. The press release literally tells you their priorities and budget availability. It is the highest-quality buying signal available.

The compounding effect: Companies that raise funding successfully often raise again 12-18 months later. Win them early in their journey and you grow with them across multiple funding stages, expanding deal size as they scale.

04

Best Practices

▼

Filtering Relevant Deals - Quality Over Quantity

▼

Not all funding is equal signal

Hundreds of companies raise funding every week. Tracking everything creates noise. Focus on funding rounds that indicate genuine buying intent for your category based on stage, amount, and stated priorities.

Filtering criteria to apply

  • Funding stage relevance - Series A and B companies are typically buying category-defining tools. Seed companies may still be too early. Series C+ companies may have already chosen vendors
  • Minimum round size - Filter for rounds above certain threshold based on your ACV. If you sell £20k ACV product, ignore sub-£1M rounds. Not enough budget for meaningful tool investment
  • Industry and sector alignment - Only track companies in industries where your solution applies. B2B SaaS tool should not waste time tracking consumer app funding
  • Geographic fit - If you only serve UK and EU markets, filter out US-only companies unless they have stated European expansion plans

Signal quality by funding stage

StageSignal QualityWhy
Seed Low-Medium Often still finding product-market fit. Limited budget for non-essential tools. May pivot. Good for land-and-expand plays
Series A Very High Building foundational infrastructure. High buying intent. Budget to invest properly. This is the sweet spot for many B2B tools
Series B High Scaling proven model. Professionalising operations. Upgrading from Seed-stage tools. Strong buying intent but may have vendor relationships
Series C+ Medium Established operations with existing vendors. Harder to displace incumbents. Best for complementary tools or new categories
Sweet spot formula: For most B2B SaaS tools, focus on Series A rounds of £5M+ in your target industry and geography. This balances signal quality with manageable volume.

Announcement Analysis - Mining Use of Funds

▼

What to extract from funding announcements

Press releases and announcement posts reveal strategic priorities explicitly. Companies tell you exactly what they plan to build, who they plan to hire, and what markets they will enter. This intelligence informs your entire approach.

Key elements to identify

  • Stated use of funds - Most announcements say "funds will be used to expand sales team" or "invest in product development." This tells you departmental budget priorities
  • Founder or exec quotes - Quotes reveal personal priorities and pain points. "We have been bottlenecked by manual processes" signals ops tool opportunity
  • Growth targets - Mentions of "triple revenue" or "expand to 50 enterprise customers" reveal ambition level and create urgency to invest in enablement
  • Market expansion plans - "Enter North American market" or "Launch enterprise tier" signal new infrastructure needs and receptiveness to supporting tools

Translating announcement language to outreach hooks

Announcement QuoteWhat It SignalsYour Positioning
Funds will accelerate sales hiring Building sales team from 5 to 20+ reps Position as sales productivity tool that helps new reps ramp faster and hit quota sooner. ROI tied to faster time-to-productivity
Expanding into enterprise segment Moving upmarket requires new processes and tools Highlight enterprise features and capabilities. Position as bridge helping them successfully move upmarket without stumbling
Scaling operations to support growth Current ops infrastructure cannot handle 3x scale Focus on operational efficiency and automation. Show how manual processes break at scale and your solution prevents that
Investing in product development Hiring engineers and building new features If you sell dev tools or product ops software, position as accelerating development velocity and shipping speed
Quote directly: Use exact phrases from announcement in your outreach. If founder says "accelerate our go-to-market motion," use "accelerate go-to-market" in your message. This linguistic mirroring creates subconscious alignment.

Outreach Timing and Messaging - Striking While Fresh

▼

The funding announcement window

Post-funding buying intent is highest in first 30-60 days when budgets are being allocated and priorities finalised. After 90 days, capital is deployed and switching cost increases. Speed matters.

Optimal outreach timeline

  • Days 1-3 after announcement - Too soon. They are fielding congratulations and press inquiries. Your sales message gets lost in noise
  • Days 4-14 after announcement - Sweet spot. Initial frenzy has died down. They are starting to allocate budget and make buying decisions. Reach out now
  • Days 15-30 after announcement - Still good window but urgency fading. Many budgets already earmarked. Can still work if you have strong positioning
  • 30+ days after announcement - Diminishing returns. Capital deployed to priorities. Harder to get attention. Better to wait for next trigger signal

Message structure for funding triggers

ElementPurposeExample
Genuine congratulations Open warmly and acknowledge their win Congrats on the Series A, James. Exciting to see Acme scaling

Avoid being salesy: Post-funding companies are bombarded with vendor pitches. Lead with value and insight rather than product pitch. Offer to help them succeed with their growth plans. Sales conversation comes later.

Multi-Touch Strategy - Nurturing Post-Funding Relationships

▼

One message is rarely enough

Founders and execs are overwhelmed post-funding. Your first message may not land. Plan multi-touch sequence that provides value at each touchpoint rather than just asking for meetings.

Multi-touch cadence and value delivery

  • Touch 1 - Week 1 - Initial congratulations plus insight or resource tied to their priorities
  • Touch 2 - Week 3 - Share relevant case study of similar company at same stage using your solution
  • Touch 3 - Week 5 - Provide tactical template or framework for challenge they mentioned in announcement
  • Touch 4 - Week 7 - Final attempt with direct value offer such as free consultation or assessment

Value delivery at each touch

TouchValue OfferedGoal
Touch 1 Scaling playbook or checklist for their stated priority Establish credibility and generosity. Show you understand their challenge
Touch 2 Case study of company like theirs that succeeded with similar approach Provide social proof and tangible example. Make success feel achievable
Touch 3 Ready-to-use template or tool they can implement immediately Deliver quick win they can use whether they buy from you or not. Build trust through utility
Touch 4 Free assessment or consultation offer Transition from value delivery to sales conversation. Low barrier to entry for first meeting
Long game mentality: If they do not respond to initial sequence, add to quarterly nurture. They may raise again in 12-18 months. Staying top of mind through ongoing value delivery positions you for next funding window.
05

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Generic congratulations with immediate pitch

Sending "Congrats on funding, want to see a demo?" feels opportunistic and transactional. Founders see through this immediately and delete.
Lead with genuine insight or value tied to their announced priorities. Reference specific detail from announcement. Build relationship before asking for meeting.

2. Reaching out too soon after announcement

Outreach on day of announcement gets buried in flood of congratulations and press inquiries. Your message disappears in noise.
Wait 4-7 days after announcement. Let initial frenzy die down. Reach out when they are transitioning from celebrating to executing on growth plans.

3. Not reading the full announcement

Just knowing they raised funding is not enough context. You miss critical details about priorities, use of funds, and strategic direction that inform positioning.
Read entire press release or LinkedIn post. Note stated use of funds, founder quotes, growth targets. Use this intelligence to craft highly contextual outreach.

4. Targeting wrong person in organisation

Reaching out to founder when you should target VP of function wastes the opportunity. Decision maker varies by what you sell and their org structure.
If they announced sales team expansion, target VP Sales or CRO. If ops investment, find COO or Head of Ops. Match your outreach to relevant decision maker.

5. One-and-done outreach approach

Founders are overwhelmed post-funding. Single message likely gets missed or deprioritised. You lose opportunity through lack of follow-up.
Plan 3-4 touch sequence over 6-8 weeks. Provide different value at each touch. Persistence with value wins where single message fails.

6. Ignoring smaller funding rounds

Focusing only on large Series A+ rounds means missing early relationships with companies that will grow into major accounts.
Track relevant seed rounds in your ICP. Even if not ready to buy today, building relationships early pays off when they raise Series A in 12-18 months.
