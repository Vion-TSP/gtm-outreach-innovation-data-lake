Tactic 9.6 - Creative / Guerrilla

Personalised LinkedIn Ads (Loom ABM)

Run hyper-targeted LinkedIn ad campaigns to specific accounts with custom creative including their company name, logo, or personalised video messages. Create ads that only employees of target companies will see, demonstrating extreme relevance and capturing attention that generic ads cannot.

Effort: Medium-High Cost: Medium-High ICP: All Decision Makers
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Most LinkedIn ads are generic and ignored. When decision-maker scrolls their feed and sees ad featuring their own company name or personalised video message addressing them directly, pattern interrupt is massive. They stop scrolling and pay attention because this is clearly not random targeting.

The psychology behind account-specific ads

  • Ego hook - Seeing your own company name or receiving video addressed to you triggers immediate interest. Humans are hardwired to notice personal references in sea of generic content
  • Effort signal - Creating custom ad creative for single account demonstrates serious interest. Prospect thinks "They invested time specifically targeting us" which creates reciprocity obligation
  • Relevance assumption - Personalised ad implies solution must be highly relevant. Prospect assumes if company is targeting them specifically, there must be strong fit worth exploring
  • Status validation - Being singled out for custom advertising makes prospect feel important. Especially effective with senior executives accustomed to VIP treatment

Why LinkedIn specifically

LinkedIn allows targeting by specific company names combined with job titles. You can run ads that only reach VP-level people at Acme, creating effectively private billboard visible only to your target buying committee. No other advertising platform offers this level of precision at business level.

The compounding effect: When multiple stakeholders at same company see personalised ads over several weeks, they start discussing it internally. "Have you seen these ads targeting our company?" creates buzz and brand awareness before sales team even reaches out.

04

Best Practices

▼

Account Selection - High Value Targets Only

▼

Justify cost with deal size

Personalised LinkedIn ads are expensive per account. Running month-long campaign targeting single company can cost several thousand pounds. This tactic only makes economic sense for high-value deals where customer lifetime value justifies aggressive customer acquisition cost.

Account viability criteria

  • Deal size threshold - Target accounts where expected deal value is at least 10x your monthly ad spend per account. If spending £2000 per month on ads for one account, need realistic shot at £20k plus deal
  • Buying committee size - Need sufficient number of target stakeholders at company to build viable LinkedIn audience. Minimum 15-20 individuals matching your ICP criteria. Too small and campaign will not reach enough people
  • Active buying signals - Recent funding, expansion, leadership changes, or public discussion of problems you solve. Timing matters; do not waste budget on accounts not currently in buying mode
  • Strategic importance - Even if deal size is smaller, some accounts are worth targeting for logo value, industry influence, or expansion potential. Factor strategic value beyond initial contract

Budget allocation framework

Account TypeMonthly BudgetCampaign DurationExpected Reach
Enterprise (500 plus employees) £2000-£5000 2-3 months Reach 30-50 decision makers multiple times
Mid-market (100-500 employees) £1000-£2000 1-2 months Reach 15-30 decision makers multiple times
SMB (under 100 employees) Not recommended - Audience too small for effective campaign

ROI reality check: Before launching campaigns, calculate customer acquisition cost ceiling you can afford based on deal size and margin. Be honest about whether personalised LinkedIn ads fit within that ceiling. This tactic is powerful but expensive.

Creative Strategy - Relevance Over Polish

▼

Personalisation that gets noticed

Goal of creative is pattern interrupt, not winning design awards. Target needs to immediately recognise ad is specifically for them. Authenticity and relevance beat glossy production values. Focus creative energy on demonstrating you understand their specific situation.

Creative approaches ranked by impact

  • Personalised video message - Highest impact. Record Loom video addressing company by name and discussing their specific challenges. Authentic and cannot be ignored when they see their company name in video thumbnail
  • Company logo plus custom headline - Strong impact. Their logo at top of ad with headline like "James, here's how Acme can reduce customer churn." Personal and company-specific
  • Industry-specific visual with company name - Good impact. Screenshot or visual showing something relevant to their industry with their company name overlaid. Shows you understand their context
  • Data or insight specific to their company - Moderate impact. Statistic or insight derived from research about their company or industry. Demonstrates expertise but less immediate pattern interrupt

Messaging framework

Message ElementPurposeExample
Company name mention Immediate pattern interrupt and personalisation signal "Acme, we built this specifically for you"
Specific pain point Demonstrate understanding of their challenges "Struggling with customer data silos across your tech stack?"
Relevant outcome Show what is possible if they solve problem "See how similar companies reduced churn while scaling revenue"
Low-friction CTA Make next step easy and non-threatening "See the 3-minute overview we built for Acme"
Video tips: For Loom videos, keep under 2 minutes. Start with their company name and specific challenge in first 10 seconds. Smile, be authentic, show your face. Overly scripted feels fake. Natural enthusiasm beats perfect delivery.

Campaign Setup - Precise Targeting

▼

LinkedIn targeting configuration

Success depends on reaching right people at target account. Too broad and you waste budget on irrelevant employees. Too narrow and you miss key decision makers. Layer targeting criteria to build precise audience of buying committee members.

Targeting layers to apply

  • Company name - Enter exact company name as it appears on LinkedIn. Verify spelling matches their LinkedIn company page. This is primary targeting criterion
  • Job titles - Include all relevant titles on buying committee such as VP Sales, Director Marketing, Head of Operations. Use LinkedIn title suggestions to find variations
  • Seniority level - Filter by seniority to focus on decision makers. Typically Director level and above for enterprise deals. Include Manager level for mid-market
  • Job functions - Supplement titles with job function categories like Sales, Marketing, IT to catch people with non-standard titles

Campaign structure options

StructureWhen to UseProsCons
One campaign per account Testing with small number of accounts Simple to manage, clear budget per account, easy reporting Does not scale well beyond 5-10 accounts, admin overhead
Campaign per industry with account-specific ads Targeting many accounts in same industry Easier campaign management, can share some creative elements Less granular control over budget per account
Campaign per buying committee role Different messages for different roles Tailored messaging by job function, test role-specific approaches Complex setup, requires multiple creative variations
Single campaign with matched audiences Advanced setup with many accounts Scalable to 50 plus accounts, automated targeting Requires matched audience upload, less transparency per account
Audience size warning: LinkedIn requires minimum audience size to launch campaign, typically 300 plus members. If your target account does not have enough employees matching your criteria, LinkedIn will not allow campaign to run. Check audience size estimate before investing in creative.

Performance Tracking - Connect Ads to Pipeline

▼

Track beyond standard metrics

LinkedIn Campaign Manager shows clicks and impressions, but real value is in identifying which individuals engaged and connecting ad exposure to sales conversations. Implement tracking that shows sales team who has seen ads before outreach call.

Essential tracking implementations

  • LinkedIn Insight Tag on landing pages - Tracks visitors from ads including company and job title. See which specific roles are clicking through from each target account
  • CRM integration - Connect ad engagement data to contact records in CRM. Sales reps see that prospect viewed ad 3 times before conversation, informing their approach
  • UTM parameters - Tag all ad links with UTM codes including account name. Track landing page visits per account in Google Analytics
  • Video view analytics - For Loom videos, track which individuals watched, how much they watched, and whether they shared. High engagement signals buying interest

Metrics hierarchy

MetricWhat It Tells YouAction Threshold
Impression frequency How many times target individuals see your ad Aim for 5-10 impressions per person per month for awareness
Click-through rate How compelling your creative and message are LinkedIn average is 0.5-1%, aim for 2% plus with personalised ads
Landing page engagement Whether visitors find value after clicking Time on page over 2 minutes signals genuine interest
Account-level engagement How many individuals from target account interacted 5 plus individuals engaging signals account-wide awareness
Pipeline influence Whether ad exposure correlates with opportunities created Track time from first ad exposure to sales conversation initiation
Attribution challenge: Linking ad exposure directly to closed deals is difficult. Focus on leading indicators like named individuals engaging, sales conversations with ad-aware prospects, and shortened sales cycles. Ads create awareness; sales closes deals.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Targeting accounts too small

Running personalised ad campaigns for companies with only 5-10 target individuals. Audience too small to meet LinkedIn minimums or justify ad spend.
Focus on mid-market and enterprise accounts with at least 15-20 buying committee members. Smaller accounts are not viable for this tactic regardless of deal size.

2. Generic creative with just company name

Adding company logo to otherwise generic ad and calling it personalised. No specific insights or relevant messaging. Feels like lazy mail merge.
Research each account deeply and reference specific challenges, initiatives, or context in creative. Personalisation must go beyond surface-level name-dropping.

3. No landing page personalisation

Personalised ad clicks through to generic landing page with no mention of target company. Experience disconnect kills conversion.
Create dedicated landing pages for each account continuing personalisation from ad. Mention company name, show relevant case studies, maintain contextual relevance.

4. Running campaigns without sales coordination

Marketing runs personalised ad campaigns but sales team has no visibility into who is engaging. Miss opportunities to leverage warm awareness in outreach.
Implement tracking that notifies sales when prospects engage with ads. Coordinate direct outreach to reference ad content and capitalise on awareness created.

5. Insufficient budget or duration

Running campaigns for only week or two with minimal budget. Not enough impressions to build awareness or saturate target buying committee.
Commit to at least 4-8 weeks per account with sufficient budget to reach target individuals 5-10 times. Awareness campaigns need time and frequency to work.

6. No performance tracking beyond clicks

Only measuring standard metrics like impressions and clicks. No visibility into which individuals engaged or impact on pipeline.
Implement LinkedIn Insight Tag, CRM integration, and video analytics. Track named individuals engaging and correlate ad exposure with sales conversations.
