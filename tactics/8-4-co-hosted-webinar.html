Tactic 8.4 - Partnerships & Ecosystem

Co-Hosted Webinar Series

Partner with complementary companies to co-host webinars combining both audiences. Split promotion efforts, share speaking time, and double your reach by accessing partner's contact base while offering more comprehensive content than either company could deliver alone.

Effort: Medium Cost: Low-Medium ICP: All Decision Makers
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Hosting webinars alone requires massive promotional effort to build attendance. Co-hosting splits that burden while doubling potential audience size. Partner promotes to their list, you promote to yours, and attendees get more valuable content from combined expertise. Everyone wins.

The psychology behind co-hosted content

  • Authority multiplication - Two companies presenting together signal more credibility than one. Attendees perceive content as more objective and comprehensive when multiple experts contribute
  • Audience trust transfer - Partner's audience trusts them but may not know you. By appearing together, their trust transfers to you. You become credible by association before saying a word
  • Reduced self-promotion perception - Solo vendor webinars feel like sales pitches. Co-hosted sessions with complementary partners feel more educational because no single company dominates. Attendees lower their guard
  • FOMO amplification - When two companies co-promote an event, it creates perception of importance. People think "If these two companies are collaborating, this must be significant content I should not miss"

Why webinars specifically

Webinars remain one of few formats where prospects give you 45-60 minutes of undivided attention. Unlike content they skim or videos they watch passively, webinar attendees are present, engaged, and open to learning. Co-hosting multiplies this opportunity by exposing you to entirely new audience who showed up because they trust your partner.

The compounding effect: Each successful webinar strengthens partner relationship and builds your reputation with their audience. Attendees who find value will seek out your content independently. Over a series of webinars, you build awareness and credibility that compounds into pipeline months later.

StepActionDetailsTime Investment
1 Identify Potential Partners Research companies serving same ICP with complementary offerings. Look for those with similar audience size and engagement levels. Create target list of 5-10 potential partners ranked by strategic fit 2-3 hours research
2 Pitch Webinar Concept Reach out to partner marketing leads with specific webinar topic that benefits both audiences. Explain how collaboration doubles reach and reduces individual promotion burden. Propose rough format and timeline 30 min per partner outreach
3 Develop Joint Content Collaborate on webinar outline, slide deck, and speaker notes. Divide speaking time roughly equally. Ensure content flows naturally between speakers and showcases both companies' expertise without being overly promotional 3-5 hours content development

Pro tip: Structure as quarterly webinar series rather than one-off event. Committing to multiple webinars with same partner lets you refine promotion strategy, build audience anticipation, and deepen partnership over time. Easier to build momentum than starting from scratch each time.

04

Best Practices

▼

Partner Selection - Complementary Not Competitive

▼

Choose partners strategically

The right co-host partner serves same ICP but solves different problem. You want complementary expertise that creates more valuable content together than either could deliver alone. Wrong partner dilutes your message or creates uncomfortable competitive dynamics during presentation.

Ideal partner characteristics

  • Same ICP, different solution - Marketing automation company plus analytics platform serving same marketing leaders. Both relevant, no direct competition
  • Sequential workflow relationship - Your solution addresses challenge that comes before or after partner's solution in typical workflow. Natural content flow
  • Similar audience size - Partner with roughly equal email list size or social following. Imbalanced partnerships feel unfair when one contributes much larger audience
  • Aligned brand positioning - If you position as premium solution, partner with other premium brands. Mismatched positioning confuses audience and dilutes brand

Partnership fit assessment

Evaluation CriteriaGreen FlagRed Flag
ICP overlap Target same job titles, company sizes, and industries Completely different buyer personas with minimal overlap
Solution relationship Complementary or sequential in customer journey Direct competitors solving exact same problem
Audience size Within 2x of each other in email subscribers or social following One partner has 10x larger audience creating imbalanced value exchange
Content quality Partner produces high-quality educational content regularly Partner only creates promotional content or rarely publishes
Vetting approach: Before formal partnership discussion, attend partner's existing webinars or review their content. Assess presentation quality, audience engagement, and whether their style aligns with yours. Chemistry between speakers matters for webinar success.

Topic Selection - Value Over Promotion

▼

Choose topics both companies can address

Best co-hosted webinar topics let each company contribute unique expertise while building toward cohesive narrative. Avoid topics where one company dominates and the other feels tacked on. Content should flow naturally between speakers, with each adding distinct value.

Topic brainstorming framework

  • Pain point both companies address differently - "How to reduce customer churn" where one speaks to onboarding, other to ongoing engagement
  • End-to-end workflow you both enable - "From lead to loyal customer" covering acquisition through retention with natural handoff between partners
  • Industry challenge requiring multiple solutions - "Scaling sales operations" where one covers tech stack, other covers process and enablement
  • Trend or change affecting shared ICP - "Preparing for cookie-less marketing" with each company explaining implications for their domain

Content structure templates

StructureUse CaseTime Split

Promotional balance: Agree upfront on promotional limits. Common rule: no more than 5 minutes of direct product pitch per company in 45-minute webinar. Focus on education and save sales messaging for follow-up communications.

Promotion Strategy - Coordinate Outreach

▼

Maximise reach through coordinated promotion

Value of co-hosting comes from accessing partner's audience. Effective promotion requires coordination on timing, messaging, and channels. Create shared promotional assets both teams can use while allowing each to customise for their brand voice and audience.

Promotional timeline

  • 4 weeks before: Save the date - Both companies announce upcoming webinar on social media and in newsletters. Light touch with topic teaser and registration link
  • 3 weeks before: Value preview - Share content previews such as key insights attendees will learn. First email campaign to relevant list segments
  • 2 weeks before: Speaker spotlight - Highlight speakers from both companies and their expertise. Second email to non-registrants
  • 1 week before: Final push - Urgency messaging about limited seats or registration deadline. Third email plus social media reminders
  • Day before: Reminder - Email confirmed registrants with joining details and what to prepare
  • Day of: Last chance - Morning-of email with one-click join link

Follow-Up Execution - Convert Attendees to Pipeline

▼

Turn webinar engagement into qualified conversations

Webinar is beginning of relationship, not end goal. Highest-value attendees need personalised follow-up within 24 hours while interest is fresh. Coordinate with partner on follow-up approach to avoid overwhelming attendees with duplicate outreach.

Attendee segmentation for follow-up

  • Engaged attendees who asked questions - Highest intent. Sales team should reach out personally within 24 hours referencing their specific question
  • Attendees who stayed for full session - Medium-high intent. Marketing follow-up with recording plus relevant resource. Sales can follow up if they match ICP
  • Attendees who left early - Lower intent but still interested enough to register. Marketing follow-up with recording and invitation to next webinar
  • Registrants who did not attend - Send recording with subject line acknowledging they missed live session. Invite to on-demand viewing

Recording strategy: Both companies should host recording on their websites with gated download. This generates additional leads post-event from organic search and social shares. Promote recording for at least 4-6 weeks after live event to maximise value.
05

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Choosing competitive partner

Partnering with company that directly competes creates awkward dynamics. Content feels forced as each tries to position themselves as superior solution.
Select complementary partners solving different problems for same ICP. Ensure natural content flow without competitive tension. Test with small collaboration before committing to webinar series.

2. Imbalanced promotion effort

One partner promotes heavily while other does minimal outreach. Promoter feels taken advantage of, non-promoter sees poor attendance from their network. Partnership sours.
Set clear promotional expectations upfront including email sends, social posts, and ad budget if applicable. Track promotion efforts from both sides. Address imbalances early.

3. Too sales-focused content

Webinar becomes dual product pitch rather than educational content. Attendees feel bait-and-switched and leave poor reviews or unsubscribe.
Agree on promotional limits upfront such as 5 minutes product mention maximum per company. Focus content on education, tactical insights, and best practices. Save sales messaging for follow-up.

4. Poor speaker chemistry

Speakers from both companies have not met before webinar. Presentation feels disjointed with awkward transitions and conflicting styles.
Schedule rehearsal call where speakers run through full content together. Adjust flow based on natural conversation. Identify transition points. Build rapport before going live.

5. No follow-up plan

Both companies assume partner will handle follow-up. Attendees receive no communication post-event. Recording is not promoted. Leads go cold.
Define follow-up ownership before webinar. Document who follows up with which attendee segments and when. Create shared timeline. Both companies should nurture leads from event.

6. One-off event with no strategy

Investing significant time in single webinar then never collaborating again. Relationship momentum is lost. Audience building starts from zero for next event.
Structure as quarterly or monthly series with same partner. Build anticipation for next session during current webinar. Compound audience growth and partnership value over time.
