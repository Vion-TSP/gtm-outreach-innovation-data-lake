Tactic 2.1 - LinkedIn-Native

LinkedIn Live AMA Sessions

Host Ask Me Anything sessions on LinkedIn Live focused on ICP pain points. High engagement format that positions you as a thought leader and generates inbound leads from attendees and viewers.

Li
LinkedIn (Primary)
Effort: Medium Cost: Free-Low ICP: Founders, Technical Buyers, Sales Leaders
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: LinkedIn Live is massively underutilised. Most companies focus on posts and articles, ignoring the native video format that LinkedIn actively promotes in the algorithm. AMAs combine thought leadership with real-time engagement, creating both immediate leads and long-tail content assets.

The psychology behind Live AMAs

  • Authority positioning - Hosting a Live session signals expertise. You're not just talking about your space, you're teaching others in it
  • Real-time interaction - Live Q&A creates reciprocity. Attendees ask questions, you provide value immediately. This builds trust faster than any static content
  • FOMO amplification - Live events create urgency. People join because they don't want to miss the conversation happening now
  • Content multiplication - One 45-minute AMA generates 10-15 short clips, a full recording, a blog recap, and potentially a podcast episode. Maximum ROI per hour invested

Why LinkedIn specifically

LinkedIn Live has algorithmic privilege. The platform notifies your entire network when you go live, not just people who follow you closely. This reach amplification makes LinkedIn Live 5-10x more effective than standard posts for the same content. Plus, LinkedIn's professional context means attendees are in work mode, not scrolling casually like on other platforms.

The format advantage: AMAs remove the pressure of perfect presentation. Unlike webinars where you need polished slides, AMAs are conversational. Questions guide the content. This makes them easier to produce and more authentic to consume.

Best Practices

▼

Topic Selection (Pain Points Not Product Pitches)

▼

How to pick a topic that attracts your ICP

Your topic determines who shows up. A product-focused topic ("How Acme solves X") attracts existing customers. A pain-focused topic ("How to solve X without Y") attracts prospects actively struggling with that pain.

Topic research process

  • Mine LinkedIn comments - Look at popular posts in your space. What questions appear repeatedly in comments? Those are your topics
  • Check community forums - Browse relevant Slack communities, Reddit threads, or industry forums. What do people ask about most?
  • Run a LinkedIn poll - Post: "Planning an AMA. Which topic would be most valuable?" with 3-4 options. Let your audience choose
  • Analyse customer support tickets - What questions do your support team answer most often? Those indicate widespread pain

Good vs bad AMA topics

Bad (Product-Focused)Good (Pain-Focused)
"How Acme automates your workflow" "How to automate manual workflows without breaking existing systems"
"Why you should use sales engagement platforms" "How to scale outbound when your team is drowning in manual work"
"Product demo and Q&A" "Common mistakes killing your cold email deliverability (and how to fix them)"
Rule of thumb: If your product name appears in the title, it's a bad AMA topic. If a competitor could host the same AMA and it would still be valuable, it's a good topic.

Promotion Cadence (2 Weeks, Daily Posts, Invite DMs)

▼

Why 2 weeks matters

Most people don't see your first announcement. They see the third or fifth reminder. Two weeks gives you enough time to build awareness without annoying your network. One week is too short for busy people to plan around. Three weeks dilutes urgency.

Promotion schedule

TimelineContent TypeExample
Day 1 (2 weeks before) Announcement post + LinkedIn event "Hosting an AMA on [topic] in 2 weeks. Drop your questions in the comments."
Day 3-4 Teaser post (share a key insight) "One mistake I see constantly: [insight]. I'll cover this + more in the AMA next week."
Day 7 (1 week before) Reminder post + guest reveal (if panel format) "1 week until the AMA. Joined by Sarah from Datadog to talk about [topic]."
Day 10-11 DM invitations to key connections Personalised DMs to 20-30 key connections: "I'm hosting an AMA on [topic]. Thought you'd find it relevant given your work at [company]."
Day 13 (1 day before) Final reminder post + agenda preview "Tomorrow at 3 PM. Here's what we'll cover: [3-4 bullet points]. Link to register in comments."
Day 14 (2 hours before) Day-of reminder "Going live in 2 hours. See you there!"
DM strategy: Personally invite 20-30 people via DM. These invites convert at 30-50% (much higher than post engagement). Target: customers, warm connections, people who've engaged with your content recently, and ICP profiles you want to connect with.

Format Tips (Panel vs Solo, Q&A Structure)

▼

Solo vs panel format

FormatBest ForProsCons
Solo Established authority, narrow topic, testing the format Full control. Easier scheduling. Lower production complexity More pressure on you. Less diverse perspectives. Smaller promotional reach
Panel (2-3 people) Broader topics, building credibility, expanding reach Shared pressure. More engaging (conversation vs monologue). Each panelist promotes to their network Scheduling complexity. Need strong moderation. Risk of one person dominating

Q&A structure best practices

  • Seed questions ready - Prepare 5-7 questions in advance in case live Q&A is slow at the start. Don't announce these are pre-written; just answer them naturally
  • Acknowledge commenters by name - "Great question from James." "Sarah asks about X." This encourages more people to comment
  • Batch similar questions - If 3 people ask about the same topic, say "Several people asking about X. Here's my take..."
  • Timebox answers - 2-3 min per question max. Longer answers lose audience attention. If complex, say "I'll follow up with a post on this."
  • Use screen share strategically - Show examples, frameworks, or data visualisations when relevant. Screen share increases retention
Moderation tip for panels: Designate one person as moderator (can be you or a third person). Moderator reads questions, directs them to specific panelists, and keeps things moving. Without a moderator, panels devolve into cross-talk.

Content repurposing checklist

  • 5-10 short clips - Extract key moments (60-90 sec each). Post over next 2-3 weeks
  • Blog recap - Transcribe key Q&As. Publish as blog post: "Top 10 Questions from Our Recent AMA"
  • Carousel post - Turn top insights into a LinkedIn carousel (10 slides). Visual content gets 2x engagement
  • Podcast episode - If you have a podcast, repackage the AMA as an episode
  • Gated asset - Offer full recording as downloadable content in exchange for email
Lead qualification tip: Prioritise follow-up with people who: (1) attended live, (2) asked multiple questions, or (3) work at target accounts. These signals indicate high intent. DM them within 24h while you're still top of mind.

Repurposing (Clips for Posts, Blog Recap, Podcast Episode)

▼

Why repurposing matters

Most of your network won't attend the live AMA (typical attendance: 2-5% of your connections). But 60-80% of your network will see at least one piece of repurposed content over the next 2-3 weeks. Repurposing multiplies reach without additional effort.

Clip creation process

  1. Watch full recording - Note timestamps of strong moments (clear insights, surprising stats, good questions)
  2. Extract 5-10 segments - Aim for 60-90 sec per clip. Each clip should stand alone (context-independent)
  3. Add captions - 85% of video on LinkedIn is watched without sound. Use tools like Descript or Kapwing for auto-captions
  4. Post schedule - One clip every 2-3 days over next 2-3 weeks. Include link to full recording in first comment

Blog recap structure

  • Intro - Context: "Last week we hosted an AMA on [topic]. Here are the top 10 questions we answered."
  • Q&A format - Each section: Question as heading, answer in 2-3 paragraphs, optional screenshot or quote
  • CTA - End with: "Missed the live session? Watch the full recording here." Plus link to book a call if relevant
Efficiency hack: Use AI transcription (Descript, Otter.ai) to generate transcript. Feed transcript to ChatGPT with prompt: "Extract 10 best Q&As from this AMA transcript and format as blog post." Edit for accuracy and tone, but save 60% of writing time.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Pitching product instead of teaching

Attendees joined for insights, not a sales pitch. The moment you pivot to product features, they tune out or leave.
Focus 100% on education. Mention your product only if directly asked. Build trust first; sales conversations come later via follow-up DMs.

2. No promotion beforehand

You announce the AMA 2 days before and wonder why only 5 people show up. Most of your network didn't see the announcement.
Promote for 2 weeks minimum. Daily posts + DM invitations to key connections. Repetition drives attendance.

3. Bad audio or video setup

Grainy video and echo-y audio scream unprofessional. Attendees leave within 5 minutes.
Test setup 24h before. Use external mic (not laptop mic). Natural light or ring light facing you. Stable internet (wired if possible). Run a test stream to check quality.

4. Not engaging with comments during live

You ignore live comments and questions. Attendees feel like they're watching a recording, not participating in a live event.
Read every comment aloud. Acknowledge people by name. Pause between answers to check for new questions. Make it conversational, not a monologue.

5. No follow-up with attendees

You hosted a great AMA, then never reached out to people who attended. They forget about you within 48 hours.
Pull attendee list within 24h. Send personalised DMs thanking them and asking about their specific challenges. This is where the sales conversations start.

6. Going too long (over 60 min)

You planned 45 min but kept going for 90 min. Attendance drops by 60-70% after the first hour.
Cap at 60 min max. Announce at the start: "We'll run for 45-60 min." Use a visible timer. If more questions, offer to continue in a follow-up post or DM.
