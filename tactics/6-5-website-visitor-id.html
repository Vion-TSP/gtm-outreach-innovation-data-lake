Tactic 6.5 - Signal-Based / Intent

Website Visitor Identification

Identify companies visiting your website, especially high-intent pages like pricing, features, or case studies. Deanonymise anonymous traffic to reveal which target accounts are researching your solution. Reach out while interest is active.

Effort: Low-Medium Cost: Medium ICP: All Buyer Personas
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Most website visitors never fill out forms or identify themselves. Visitor identification technology deanonymises this traffic, revealing which companies are researching you. Pricing page visitors especially signal high buying intent. Your outreach catches them mid-evaluation.

The psychology behind website visitor signals

  • Revealed preference through behaviour - Visiting your pricing page is stronger signal than opening cold email. They actively sought you out and invested time learning about your solution
  • Self-qualification - By the time someone reaches pricing or features pages, they have typically self-qualified as having the problem you solve. They are serious prospects not casual browsers
  • Privacy-respecting engagement - People appreciate when you help them with research they are already doing versus interrupting them cold. Outreach based on website visit feels helpful not intrusive
  • Timing advantage - Catching them during active research means your outreach arrives when they are forming vendor shortlist. You influence evaluation criteria early

Why website visitor ID specifically

Unlike paid intent data from third parties, website visitors came to you directly. They found your site through search, referral, or direct navigation. This demonstrates higher motivation and more advanced buying stage than passive content consumption.

The compounding effect: Visitor identification works passively in background. Every qualified visitor becomes outreach opportunity automatically. Unlike active prospecting that requires ongoing effort, this scales with your inbound traffic.

Best Practices

▼

Page-Level Intent Scoring - Not All Visits Equal

▼

Different pages signal different intent levels

Someone reading blog post is far weaker signal than someone viewing pricing page three times. Build page-based intent scoring to prioritise outreach on highest-intent visitors.

Intent levels by page type

  • Very high intent - Pricing, demo request, comparison pages - These visitors are actively evaluating vendors and comparing options. Bottom of funnel behaviour. Immediate outreach priority
  • High intent - Product pages, case studies, integration docs - Learning how product works and whether it solves their problem. Serious consideration stage. Strong outreach opportunity
  • Medium intent - Features overview, use case pages - Understanding what product does and who it serves. Early evaluation stage. Worth nurturing but lower urgency
  • Low intent - Blog, about page, careers - General research or unrelated browsing. Weak buying signal. Skip outreach unless combined with other signals

Engagement depth scoring

BehaviourIntent SignalAction Priority
Multiple visits to pricing in one session Very High Immediate outreach same day. Actively comparing pricing models. Ready for sales conversation
Visited product pages plus case studies High Outreach within 24 hours. Evaluating product fit and social proof. Good timing for helpful conversation
Single page visit under 30 seconds Low Skip outreach. Likely bounce or misclick. Not genuine research behaviour
Return visitor over multiple days Very High Priority outreach. Persistent interest signals strong intent. They are seriously considering you
Scoring formula: Combine page type, time on page, number of pages viewed, and return visits into composite intent score. Only reach out to top 20% highest scoring visitors to maintain quality.

ICP Qualification - Filtering the Noise

▼

Not every visitor is a prospect

Visitor ID tools show all company traffic including competitors, partners, agencies, and personal research. Apply strict ICP filter before investing time in outreach.

Visitors to filter out

  • Competitors and partners - Companies in your space doing market research. Build blocklist of known competitors and partner companies to auto-filter
  • Agencies and consultants - Researching on behalf of clients or generally learning market. Rarely have buying authority themselves
  • Too small or too large - Companies outside your sweet spot company size. Filter by employee count or revenue to match ICP
  • Wrong geography - If you only serve specific regions, filter out companies in geographies you cannot support
  • Wrong industry - Visitors from industries where your solution does not apply. Build positive filter for target industries

Quick qualification checklist

CriterionCheckReject If
Company size Employee count from LinkedIn or visitor ID tool Outside your target range (eg under 50 or over 500 depending on ICP)
Industry relevance Company description and sector Industry where your product does not apply or has no success stories
Geography HQ location from visitor IP and company data Region you do not serve or cannot support due to time zone or compliance

Auto-filtering: Build ICP filter rules directly in visitor ID tool if possible. This prevents unqualified visitors from appearing in daily reports, saving manual filtering time.

Outreach Messaging - Page-Specific Hooks

▼

Tailor message to pages they viewed

Generic "saw you visited our site" misses opportunity to reference specific pages and demonstrate understanding of what they were researching. Customise hook based on their browsing behaviour.

Message templates by page type

  • Pricing page visitor - "Noticed someone from Acme was checking out our pricing. Happy to walk through how we structure plans and what makes sense for your size company."
  • Case study reader - "Saw you were looking at our case study with similar company. Can share more detail on how we helped them with X challenge if relevant."
  • Integration page visitor - "Noticed interest in our Salesforce integration. Most companies ask about Y and Z. Happy to explain how integration works."
  • Return visitor - "Noticed your team has checked us out a few times this week. Guessing you are evaluating options. Happy to answer questions that came up during research."

Message framework by intent level

Intent LevelOpening HookCTA
Very High (pricing, demo) Saw you were checking out pricing. Happy to walk through options... 15 min call to discuss your situation and what fits best?
High (features, cases) Noticed you were researching how we handle X use case... Can share more context or answer specific questions if helpful?
Medium (general product) Saw someone from Acme was learning about our platform... Here is buyer guide we created. Let me know if questions come up
Transparency approach: Some companies worry about revealing visitor tracking. Test being explicit: "We use visitor ID technology to see when companies research us. Saw Acme visited our site." Honesty often builds trust.

Timing and Response Speed - Strike While Fresh

▼

Website visitor signals decay quickly

Unlike funding announcements that stay relevant for weeks, website visit intent degrades within days. Visitor researched you today. Tomorrow they may research competitor. Speed wins.

Optimal response timeline

  • Same day ideal - Real-time alerts let you reach out hours after visit. Your brand is fresh. They may still be actively researching. Best conversion timing
  • Within 24 hours strong - Daily morning review lets you contact yesterday visitors. Still recent enough for good brand recall. Solid response window
  • 24-48 hours acceptable - Slightly degraded but worth attempting. They may have moved to other vendors but not decided yet
  • 48+ hours weak - Intent signal mostly decayed. They likely already shortlisted vendors or moved on. Low conversion probability

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Treating all page visits equally

Blog reader and pricing page visitor have completely different intent levels. Generic "saw you visited our site" approach misses opportunity to prioritise high-intent signals.
Build page-level intent scoring. Prioritise outreach to pricing and demo page visitors. Filter out low-intent blog and career page traffic entirely.

2. Slow response to visitor signals

Checking visitor dashboard once per week means signals are 5-7 days old. Visitor already researched competitors and made shortlist. Window closed.
Review visitor dashboard daily at minimum. Set up real-time Slack alerts for high-intent pages. Reach out same day or within 24 hours maximum.

3. No ICP qualification filtering

Reaching out to every visitor includes competitors, wrong company sizes, irrelevant industries. Waste time on unqualified opportunities.
Apply strict ICP filter before outreach. Build blocklist of competitors. Filter by company size and industry. Only contact qualified prospects.

4. Generic messaging ignoring pages viewed

Saying "saw you visited our website" without referencing specific pages feels generic and surveillance-like. Miss chance to demonstrate understanding.
Reference specific pages they viewed. "Saw you were checking out pricing" or "noticed you read our case study on X." Make it obviously personalised.

5. Leading with aggressive sales pitch

They came to your site to research independently. Immediate hard sell feels pushy and disrespects their buying process. They disengage.
Position as helpful resource for their evaluation. Offer to answer questions or provide additional context. Support their research rather than pressuring for demo.

6. Not enriching visitor data with context

Knowing company name is not enough. Without understanding their size, industry, recent activity, messaging feels generic and conversion suffers.
Research each qualified visitor before outreach. Check LinkedIn for company info, recent funding, job postings. Use context to craft highly relevant message.
