Tactic 7.2 - ABM Plays

Custom Video for Target Accounts

Record 60-90 second personalised videos for high-value prospects showing specific analysis of their business, website, or competitive positioning. Demonstrates effort and expertise while creating emotional connection that text cannot achieve.

Effort: High Cost: Low ICP: C-suite, Founders
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Video creates human connection impossible through text. When someone sees your face, hears your voice, and watches you analyse their specific business, they feel the investment you made. This emotional response combined with demonstrated expertise dramatically increases response rates compared to written outreach.

The psychology behind custom videos

  • Effort visibility - Text can be templated and automated. Video clearly required personal time investment. Prospects immediately recognise and appreciate this effort, creating reciprocity pressure
  • Credibility through demonstration - Talking through specific insights about their business proves expertise more effectively than claiming expertise in writing. Show rather than tell
  • Emotional connection - Seeing a real person creates trust and likeability that text cannot replicate. Humans are wired to respond to faces and voices
  • Curiosity trigger - Subject line "I recorded this for you, Sarah" creates powerful curiosity. What did they record? What did they find? Email gets opened

Why video specifically for high-value accounts

Custom video does not scale and should not scale. Recording personalised videos for hundreds of prospects would be inefficient and quality would suffer. This tactic works precisely because it is reserved for your top tier targets where the potential deal value justifies the time investment.

The pattern interrupt: C-suite executives receive thousands of emails weekly. Most are text. A personalised video immediately stands out in the inbox. Even if they have seen video prospecting before, a genuinely custom analysis of their business is rare enough to capture attention.

Best Practices

▼

Research and Preparation - Show Their Actual Data

▼

What makes research valuable for video

Generic insights anyone could find in 5 minutes on Google do not justify a custom video. Your research should uncover specific observations about their business that demonstrate both expertise and effort. This is what transforms a video from gimmick to genuine value-add.

Research areas that translate well to video

  • Website analysis - Walk through their website while screen sharing. Point out specific pages, messaging, CTAs, or technical elements relevant to what you solve. "I noticed on your pricing page that..." creates specific credibility
  • Competitive positioning - Show how they position against competitors using their own website and competitor sites side by side. Highlight gaps or opportunities in their positioning
  • Tech stack insights - Use BuiltWith or similar to identify tools they use. Explain integration opportunities or gaps in their stack that you address
  • Content and messaging audit - Review their recent blog posts, LinkedIn content, or case studies. Share observations about messaging consistency or opportunities to strengthen their story

Research checklist before recording

Research ItemWhere to Find ItHow to Use in Video
Recent company news Company blog, press releases, LinkedIn posts from executives "Saw your recent announcement about X. This likely means you are dealing with Y challenge..."
Website structure and messaging Browse entire website, read all key pages Screen share specific page and point out element: "Your value prop here says X, which suggests..."
Competitor comparison Visit 2-3 main competitor websites "Compared to how your competitors position on this, you have opportunity to differentiate by..."
LinkedIn presence Target prospect LinkedIn profile and recent activity "Noticed you posted about X last week. That aligns with what we are seeing across your industry..."
Depth indicator: If you could deliver the same insights after researching any company in their industry, your research is too shallow. Aim for observations that are unique to their specific company, not generic industry commentary.

Video Structure - 60-90 Seconds Maximum

▼

Why length matters critically

Busy executives will watch a 60 second video. They will not watch 5 minutes. Every second beyond 90 seconds dramatically decreases completion rate. A video they watch completely is infinitely more valuable than a longer video they abandon after 20 seconds.

Optimal video structure

  • 0-10 seconds: Personalised hook - "Hi Sarah, I am James. I spent some time looking at Acme and wanted to share a few quick thoughts." Establish who you are and why this is relevant to them specifically
  • 10-60 seconds: Core insights - Share 2-3 specific observations while screen sharing their website or relevant data. Focus on insights not sales pitch. Demonstrate expertise through analysis
  • 60-75 seconds: Bridge to value - Brief transition explaining how these observations relate to what you do: "This is exactly the kind of challenge we help companies like Acme solve."
  • 75-90 seconds: Clear call-to-action - Specific next step: "If this resonates, grab 15 minutes on my calendar." Include calendar link in email accompanying video

Common timing mistakes

MistakeWhy It FailsBetter Approach
Long introduction about yourself They do not care about your background yet. They care if you understand their business 5 seconds maximum on who you are. Jump immediately into insights about them
Explaining all product features Sounds like sales pitch. They stop watching. Plus 90 seconds is not enough time anyway Mention what you do in one sentence. Save product detail for the conversation you are trying to earn
Rambling without script outline Loses focus and goes long. Viewer loses interest Outline key points before recording. Practice once to ensure timing. Then record for real
Editing permission: If your first take goes 2 minutes, do not send it. Re-record with tighter focus. Or use editing to cut it down to 90 seconds. Sending a long rambling video signals you do not respect their time.

Delivery Quality - Natural Not Scripted

▼

The authenticity balance

A perfectly polished video feels corporate and impersonal. A sloppy video signals lack of professionalism. The goal is authentic expertise, which means conversational delivery with good production quality on basics like lighting and audio.

Production quality checklist

  • Lighting - Natural light from window facing you, or simple ring light. Avoid backlighting where window is behind you making you a dark silhouette
  • Audio - Use external mic or earbuds, not laptop mic. Clear audio is more important than perfect video. People tolerate mediocre video but abandon videos with bad audio
  • Background - Clean, professional background. Home office is fine. Messy room or distracting elements harm credibility. Use virtual background only if your real background is problematic
  • Eye contact - Look at camera not at your own image on screen. This creates eye contact feeling for viewer. Small adjustment with massive impact on connection

Delivery style

AvoidAim For
Reading from script word-for-word sounding robotic Speaking from bullet point outline with natural conversational delivery
Overly formal corporate language Talking like you would explain to a colleague over coffee
Monotone delivery with no energy Genuine enthusiasm about the insights you are sharing, but not fake hype
Filler words every sentence - um, uh, like, you know Occasional filler word is human and fine. Excessive fillers undermine credibility
Retake permission: If you stumble badly or lose your train of thought, stop and start over. It takes 90 seconds to record. Spending 5 minutes doing 3 takes to get a clean version is time well invested. But do not obsess over perfection or you will never send any videos.

Follow-Up Strategy - Track Views and Act Fast

▼

Why view tracking matters

Someone who watches your video but does not reply is not a lost opportunity. They are showing interest signal through behaviour even without explicit response. View data tells you who is engaged and deserves prioritised follow-up.

Interpreting view signals

View BehaviourSignal StrengthFollow-Up Action
No view within 48 hours Low - email may not have been opened or video ignored Send follow-up email with different subject line or try different contact at same company
Watched under 30%, single view Medium - they checked it out but did not finish. Mild curiosity Wait 5-7 days then send follow-up email offering different resource or angle
Watched 50-90%, single view High - engaged with content even if did not respond Call or personalised email within 24 hours: "Saw you watched the video. Would love to discuss X in more detail."
Multiple views or shared internally Very high - showing to colleagues or reviewing multiple times before responding Immediate call or very personalised follow-up within 12 hours. They are seriously considering

Follow-up email template after view

Subject: Re: Video for Sarah at Acme

Sarah,

Noticed you watched the video I sent over. The point about how your pricing page could better highlight ROI for enterprise buyers seemed to resonate based on what I know about Acme's growth plans.

Happy to walk through that in more detail or share examples of how similar companies have approached this. Worth 15 minutes this week?

Grab time here if so: [calendar link]

Speed matters: Following up within 24 hours of a view significantly increases response rate compared to waiting several days. They watched your video, you are top of mind, strike while the interest is hot.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Video too long - over 2 minutes

Busy executives will not watch a 5 minute prospecting video no matter how good the content. Completion rate drops dramatically after 90 seconds.
Ruthlessly edit to 60-90 seconds maximum. Focus on 2-3 key insights only. Save detailed explanation for the meeting you are trying to earn. Practice and time yourself before recording.

2. Reading from script - sounds robotic

When you read word-for-word from script you sound unnatural and disengaged. Eye contact is wrong, delivery is flat, credibility suffers.
Outline key points in bullets, not full sentences. Practice once to know your flow. Then record speaking naturally to camera as if explaining to a colleague. Conversational beats polished.

3. Not showing their actual website or data

Just talking to camera without visuals makes it harder to prove genuine customisation. Could be template with name swapped.
Screen share their website, LinkedIn profile, or competitor comparison while talking. Point to specific elements. Visual proof plus verbal analysis demonstrates authentic research and custom effort.

4. Generic value prop despite video format

Recording a video does not automatically make content valuable. If insights are generic industry commentary anyone could give, the format does not save it.
Every insight should be specific to their company not just their industry. Use their company name, their website pages, their specific context. Generic insights in video format still fail.

5. No follow-up on video views

Prospect watches your video showing interest, but you never follow up. They move on, interest fades, opportunity lost.
Check view analytics daily. When someone watches your video, follow up within 24 hours while you are fresh in their mind. Video view is warm signal deserving prioritised outreach.

6. Poor audio or lighting quality

Bad audio is unwatchable. People abandon videos with poor sound even if content is good. Dark video or distracting background harms credibility.
Invest in basic external mic or use earbuds. Face window for natural light or use simple ring light. Test audio and video before recording. Clean background. Production basics are table stakes.
