Tactic 10.3 - AI-Powered

AI Voice Agents

Deploy AI voice agents to make outbound calls, qualify leads, answer questions, handle objections, and book meetings automatically. AI handles conversational phone outreach at scale while sounding natural and human-like.

Effort: Medium Cost: Medium-High ICP: Founders, Sales Leaders, Operations Teams
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Phone conversations convert better than emails but do not scale economically. AI voice agents solve this by conducting human-quality phone conversations at machine scale and cost. You get phone channel effectiveness with email channel economics.

The psychology behind AI voice effectiveness

  • Immediate engagement advantage - Phone conversation forces immediate attention. Cannot ignore or delay like email. Prospect must engage in real-time creating urgency
  • Natural conversation builds trust - Voice conversation feels more personal and trustworthy than text. Tone and pacing convey warmth. Human connection happens faster
  • Real-time objection handling - AI responds to objections immediately in conversation. No delay for follow-up email. Maintains momentum and addresses concerns before they solidify
  • Qualification through dialogue - Conversational format lets AI ask clarifying questions naturally. Discovers true needs and pain points through back-and-forth that email cannot achieve

Why AI voice agents specifically

Human SDRs making 50 calls per day is expensive and does not scale. Recordings and IVR feel robotic and impersonal. AI voice agents deliver natural conversation quality at infinite scale with zero incremental cost per call.

The compounding effect: AI voice improves through machine learning. Every call trains the model. Objection handling gets better. Tone becomes more natural. Quality increases as volume scales.

Best Practices

▼

Script and Voice Configuration - Sounding Natural

▼

Script quality determines conversation quality

AI can only work within the script you provide. Rigid scripting produces robotic conversations. Good scripting creates natural dialogue with flexibility to adapt to prospect responses.

Script structure best practices

  • Conversational opening - Start naturally like human would. "Hi James, this is Sarah calling from Acme. Do you have a quick minute?" Not formal script reading
  • Clear value proposition early - State why you are calling within first 15 seconds. "Calling about X because companies like yours often struggle with Y." Give reason to stay on call
  • Question-based flow - Use questions to guide conversation not monologue. "What are you currently using for X?" AI adapts responses based on answers
  • Branching logic for responses - Script multiple paths for different objections and responses. "If they say not interested, say X. If they ask about pricing, say Y." Handles variations

Voice persona selection

Voice TypeBest ForCharacteristics
Professional female General B2B outbound to executives Warm but professional tone. Clear articulation. Perceived as trustworthy and competent. Good general purpose choice
Professional male Technical products or male-dominated industries Authoritative tone. Good for technical discussions. May be preferred in certain industries or cultures
Friendly casual SMB outreach or less formal industries More relaxed tone. Approachable. Works well for small business owners who prefer casual conversation
Custom voice clone Founder-led sales or when brand voice matters Clone of actual team member voice. Most authentic. Requires voice samples to train. Best for relationship-driven sales
Iteration mindset: Initial script will not be perfect. Review call recordings weekly. Identify where AI struggles or sounds unnatural. Refine script iteratively based on real conversations.

Qualification Logic - Efficient Lead Scoring

▼

AI must know what questions to ask

Qualification separates interested prospects from poor fits. Configure AI to ask right questions in natural conversation flow to determine fit, budget, authority, need, and timeline.

Qualification framework questions

  • Fit questions - "How many people on your sales team?" or "What CRM are you currently using?" Confirms they match ICP profile
  • Pain point questions - "What is your biggest challenge with X right now?" Validates problem you solve exists for them
  • Authority questions - "Are you the person who makes decisions about X?" or "Who else would be involved in evaluating solutions?" Identifies decision making process
  • Timeline questions - "When are you looking to have this solved by?" or "Are you actively evaluating options now?" Gauges urgency

Scoring and routing logic

Qualification LevelCriteriaAI Action
Hot lead ICP fit, active pain, decision maker, urgent timeline Book meeting immediately. Route to senior sales rep. Send instant notification to team
Warm lead ICP fit, identified pain, some authority, 3-6 month timeline Book discovery call. Add to nurture sequence. Assign to SDR for follow-up
Cool lead ICP fit but no immediate pain or timeline Add to long-term nurture. Send educational content. Re-engage in 6 months
Disqualified Wrong company size, no budget, wrong industry Politely end call. Mark as disqualified in CRM. Do not re-contact
Adaptive questioning: AI should ask different depth of questions based on engagement level. Interested prospects get full qualification. Hesitant prospects get lighter touch to avoid overwhelming them.

Objection Handling - Preparing AI Responses

▼

Common objections need scripted responses

Prospects will raise predictable objections. AI must have prepared responses that sound natural and address concerns without sounding defensive or salesy.

Standard objections and responses

  • Not interested - "I completely understand. Can I ask what specifically does not fit? Many companies initially think that but find X once they learn more about Y."
  • No time right now - "Totally get it. This will only take 2 minutes. I am calling because companies like yours often struggle with X. Does that resonate?"
  • Send me information - "Happy to send materials. Before I do, let me ask one quick question so I send the right info. Are you currently using anything for X?"
  • Already have solution - "Great that you have something in place. What made you choose that tool? Most of our customers came from X and found they were missing Y."

Objection response frameworks

Objection TypeResponse FrameworkGoal
Timing objection Acknowledge + Reframe urgency I understand timing is tight. Quick question - is X challenge costing you time or money right now? Often waiting costs more than acting
Budget objection Acknowledge + ROI reframe Budget is always a consideration. Our customers typically see X return within Y months. Would you be open to seeing how the economics work?
Authority objection Qualify decision process Makes sense. Who else would be involved in evaluating this? Would it be helpful if I spoke with them as well or sent materials you can share?
Competitor objection Acknowledge + differentiate We work with many companies who use X. The main difference is Y. Would it be worth 10 minutes to see if that applies to your situation?
Graceful exit: If prospect clearly not interested after objection handling, AI should exit politely. "No problem. If anything changes in future, feel free to reach out." Do not push past clear rejection.

Transparency and Disclosure - Ethics of AI Calling

▼

Should AI identify itself as AI

Voice AI quality is now good enough to pass as human in many conversations. This raises ethical question of whether AI should disclose its nature or attempt to pass as human caller.

Disclosure approaches and trade-offs

  • No disclosure - AI presents as human caller. Maximises engagement. Risk of backlash if discovered. May violate regulations in some jurisdictions
  • Upfront disclosure - "Hi, this is Sarah, an AI assistant from Acme." Fully transparent but may cause immediate hang-ups from people uncomfortable with AI
  • Disclosure on request - AI identifies as AI if directly asked but does not volunteer information. Balances transparency with effectiveness
  • Hybrid approach - AI handles initial screening, hands off to human for substantive conversation. Transparent about AI doing initial qualification

Regulatory and ethical considerations

FactorGuidanceRationale
Legal requirements Check local regulations. Some jurisdictions require AI disclosure California and EU have stricter rules. Violating can result in fines and brand damage. Better safe than sorry
Brand positioning Innovation-focused brands may embrace AI disclosure Transparency reinforces innovative positioning. Can be differentiator rather than liability
Audience sophistication Technical audiences more likely to spot AI. Disclose preemptively Being caught in deception with technical buyers destroys trust. Transparency builds credibility
Relationship stage New cold outreach versus existing relationships People you already know will recognise AI voice. Disclosure prevents awkward conversations
Recommendation: Disclosure on request balances ethics and effectiveness. AI does not volunteer that it is AI, but if asked directly responds honestly. Test both approaches and let conversion data inform decision while respecting regulations.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Overly scripted robotic conversations

Rigid script with no flexibility makes AI sound like IVR system. Prospects hang up immediately sensing automation.
Build conversational script with branching logic. Allow AI to adapt responses based on what prospect says. Sound natural not scripted.

2. No objection handling preparation

AI encounters objection it was not trained for and fumbles response. Awkward pause or generic answer. Prospect hangs up.
Script responses to 10-15 most common objections. Review call recordings to identify objections AI struggles with. Iteratively improve objection library.

3. Calling poor quality lists

Wrong numbers, disconnected lines, or poor ICP fit wastes AI calling capacity on unproductive conversations.
Verify and clean phone lists before uploading to AI. Confirm ICP fit. Remove duplicates and bad numbers. Quality list quality improves AI performance.

4. No qualification logic configured

AI books meetings with everyone regardless of fit. Sales team wastes time on unqualified leads. Efficiency destroyed.
Configure clear qualification criteria. AI asks specific questions to determine fit, budget, authority, timeline. Only qualified prospects get meetings booked.

5. Ignoring call recording insights

Sending AI to make calls without reviewing how conversations actually go means missing opportunities to improve script and handling.
Listen to random sample of calls weekly. Identify patterns where AI succeeds or struggles. Refine script based on real conversation data.

6. Not respecting time zones and calling hours

AI calling prospects at 8pm their time or 7am on Saturday creates terrible first impression. Damages brand.
Configure calling hours to respect time zones. Only call during business hours local to prospect. Include do-not-call list compliance. Professional timing matters.
