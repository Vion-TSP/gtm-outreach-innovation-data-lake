Tactic 6.7 - Signal-Based / Intent

Leadership Change Alerts

Monitor new executive and VP-level appointments at target accounts. New leaders have 90-day mandate to make their mark, often through infrastructure and tooling changes. Reach out during honeymoon period when they are actively evaluating stack.

Effort: Low Cost: Medium ICP: C-suite, VP-level Decision Makers
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: New executives are hired to drive change. They have explicit mandate and pressure to show impact quickly. Tech stack evaluation happens in first 90 days as they assess what they inherited and what they need. Your timing aligns perfectly with their evaluation window.

The psychology behind leadership change signals

  • Fresh perspective advantage - New leaders are not attached to existing vendors. No sunk cost bias or emotional investment in current tools. They evaluate with fresh eyes
  • Prove-yourself pressure - First 90 days set tone for tenure. New execs need quick wins to build credibility. Introducing better tools is visible high-impact change
  • Budget and authority - Companies hiring VP or C-level have usually allocated budget for that function. New leader controls purse strings and decision-making
  • Relationship building opportunity - Helping new leader succeed early creates long-term partnership. They remember vendors who helped them win in critical first months

Why leadership changes specifically

Unlike passive intent signals, leadership changes are concrete events with predictable evaluation timelines. You know they will assess infrastructure in first 30-90 days. Your outreach arrives exactly when they are receptive.

The compounding effect: Executives change companies every 2-4 years on average. Win them at one company and they become advocates when they move to next role. You gain multi-company champion.

Best Practices

▼

Role Selection - Which Changes to Track

▼

Not all leadership changes are equal signals

New CFO signals different buying behaviour than new CRO. Track roles with direct authority over your category and budget to purchase your type of solution.

Roles to monitor by product category

  • Sales tools - CRO, VP Sales, VP Revenue Operations, Head of Sales Development. These roles own sales infrastructure budget and decision-making
  • Marketing tools - CMO, VP Marketing, VP Growth, Head of Demand Gen. Marketing function leaders evaluate and purchase marketing stack
  • Operations tools - COO, VP Operations, Head of Business Operations. Ops leaders consolidate and optimise operational infrastructure
  • Developer tools - CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Platform. Technical leaders evaluate and standardise development tooling

Signal strength by role level

Role LevelSignal QualityWhy
C-level (CRO, CMO, CTO) Very High Ultimate budget authority. Hired to transform function. Highest willingness to change infrastructure. Direct decision maker
VP-level (VP Sales, VP Ops) High Strong influence over stack. Often given budget for their function. Can champion purchases through approval process
Director-level (Dir Sales Ops) Medium Evaluate tools but typically need VP or C-level approval for purchase. Good for influence but less direct authority
Manager-level Low Rarely have budget authority or vendor selection power. Skip unless selling very low ACV product
Relevance test: Before adding role to tracking, ask: "Does this person have authority to evaluate and purchase our category of solution?" If answer is not clear yes, filter it out. Focus on decision makers.

Timing Strategy - The 30-60 Day Window

▼

New exec journey timeline

Leadership onboarding follows predictable pattern. Understanding this lets you time outreach for maximum receptiveness when they are actively evaluating infrastructure.

Typical new exec timeline

  • Days 1-30 - Learning and listening - Meeting team, understanding current state, identifying problems. Not ready for vendor conversations yet. Too busy absorbing information
  • Days 30-60 - Evaluating and planning - Identified gaps and priorities. Actively researching solutions. Perfect window for vendor outreach. Receptive to conversations
  • Days 60-90 - Decision and implementation - Making vendor selections and beginning implementations. Window closing as decisions finalise
  • Days 90+ - Execution mode - Stack largely decided. Focused on driving results with chosen tools. Switching cost now high. Window closed

Outreach timing by tenure

Days in RoleOutreach StrategyMessage Focus
1-15 days Skip outreach Too early. They are still learning. Message gets ignored or feels pushy. Wait
30-45 days Primary outreach window Congratulations on role. Here is how we help leaders in your position deliver quick wins in first 90 days
45-60 days Secondary window if missed first More urgent positioning. Time-sensitive offer to help them hit 90-day goals before window closes
60-90 days Last chance outreach Very direct. Assume they are finalising decisions. Offer to be included in final evaluation before they commit elsewhere
90+ days Skip or long-term nurture Evaluation window closed. Add to general nurture for potential re-evaluation in 6-12 months
Calendar automation: When you identify new hire, immediately set calendar reminder for day 35 of their tenure. This ensures you do not miss optimal outreach window during busy weeks.

Research and Context Building - Know Their Mandate

▼

Understanding why they were hired

New executives are brought in to solve specific problems. Understanding their mandate lets you position directly against their priorities and demonstrate how you enable their success.

Research sources for context

  • LinkedIn announcement post - Company or exec often posts about new role with stated priorities. "Excited to join Acme to scale our enterprise sales motion" reveals mandate
  • Company press release - Larger hires get press releases explaining why person was brought in. Explicit statement of what they are tasked with accomplishing
  • Previous role and company - LinkedIn history shows what they built before. If they scaled sales ops at previous company, they will likely do same at new one
  • Recent company news - Funding rounds, expansion announcements, or strategic shifts provide context for why this hire was made now

Inferring mandate from role and company stage

ScenarioLikely MandateYour Positioning
First CRO hire at Series A Build scalable sales infrastructure from scratch Position as foundational tool that prevents growing pains. Help them build right way from start
New VP Sales replacing departed one Fix broken sales process or underperformance Highlight how you address common sales challenges. Offer fresh start with better tooling
VP Ops hired after funding round Professionalise operations to support growth Focus on operational efficiency and scaling infrastructure. Enable them to deploy capital effectively
CTO joining from larger company Bring enterprise-grade practices and tooling Emphasise enterprise features and scalability. Help them elevate technical maturity
Quote their words: If exec posted about priorities on LinkedIn, quote them directly in outreach. "You mentioned wanting to scale enterprise sales motion. Here is how we help leaders do exactly that." Shows you paid attention.

Outreach Messaging - Welcome and Enable Success

▼

Positioning as success partner

New execs are under pressure to prove themselves quickly. Position your solution as accelerator of their success in critical first 90 days. Help them win and build long-term relationship.

Message framework for new exec outreach

  • Warm congratulations - "Congrats on joining Acme as CRO. Exciting move from what I can see of your background"
  • Acknowledge their mandate - "Saw you were brought in to scale enterprise sales. That is exactly the challenge most of our customers hired us to solve"
  • Offer first-90-day value - "Most new sales leaders we work with find X and Y helpful in their first quarter to show quick wins"
  • Low-pressure CTA - "Happy to share how we have helped other CROs in similar situation. 15 min call when you come up for air?"

Positioning angles by mandate type

MandateYour AngleValue Prop
Build infrastructure from scratch Foundational best practices We help you build it right from day one. Avoid mistakes we have seen others make. Faster path to scalable infrastructure
Fix underperformance Turnaround enabler Most leaders brought in to fix sales find the problem is X. Our platform addresses root cause and drives quick improvement
Scale existing success Growth accelerator Scaling from 10 to 50 reps breaks without right infrastructure. We help you maintain quality and control through rapid growth
Professionalise operations Maturity upgrade Moving from scrappy to enterprise-grade requires different tooling. We bridge that gap without massive disruption
First 90 days framing: Always frame value in context of their critical first quarter. "Help you deliver quick wins in first 90 days" resonates because it acknowledges prove-yourself pressure they feel.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Reaching out too early in their tenure

Contacting them first week when they are drowning in onboarding means message gets ignored. They are not ready for vendor conversations yet.
Wait 30-45 days before outreach. Give them time to assess current state and identify gaps. Then reach out during active evaluation window days 30-60.

2. Generic congratulations with no context

Saying "Congrats on new role, let me show you our product" feels opportunistic. No connection to their priorities or mandate. Gets deleted.
Research their background and stated priorities. Connect your solution specifically to their mandate. Show you understand why they were hired and how you help them succeed.

3. Tracking irrelevant role changes

Monitoring every leadership change creates noise. New CFO or new Head of People rarely signal need for sales or marketing tools. Waste time on poor-fit signals.
Only track roles with direct authority over your category. CRO for sales tools, CMO for marketing tools, CTO for dev tools. Filter by buyer persona relevance.

4. Waiting too long to reach out

Reaching out 90+ days into tenure means they have already evaluated and chosen vendors. Stack decisions are made. Window closed.
Set calendar reminder for day 35 of their tenure. Reach out days 30-60 maximum. Missing this window means waiting 6-12 months for re-evaluation cycle.

5. Not understanding their mandate

Pitching generic value proposition without connecting to why they were hired misses opportunity. They care about accomplishing their specific goals not your features.
Research announcement posts, press releases, and previous roles. Infer what they were tasked with accomplishing. Position directly against their mandate.

6. Treating all exec changes equally

First-time CRO building from scratch has different needs than replacement CRO fixing broken sales. Same role, different context requires different positioning.
Understand company stage and whether role is new, replacement, or promotion. Tailor messaging to their specific situation and challenges they inherited.
