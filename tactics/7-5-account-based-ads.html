Tactic 7.5 - ABM Plays

Account-Based Advertising

Run LinkedIn and display ads targeted specifically at employees of high-value target accounts. Reach entire buying committee with coordinated messaging while sales team conducts direct outreach, creating multi-channel touchpoint strategy that builds awareness and credibility.

Effort: Medium Cost: Medium-High ICP: All Buying Committee Members
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Cold outreach feels cold when it is the first time someone has heard of you. Account-based ads warm up target accounts before direct outreach, creating familiarity and credibility. When your sales team reaches out, prospects think "I have been seeing them everywhere" rather than "who is this random person?"

The psychology behind account-based ads

  • Mere exposure effect - People prefer things they have seen before. When prospects see your ads for weeks before getting your email, you feel familiar rather than unknown. Familiarity breeds trust
  • Social proof amplification - Seeing ads for a company signals that company has resources and legitimacy. Startups nobody heard of do not run ads. Established players do. Ads create perception of stability and market presence
  • Omnichannel reinforcement - Seeing your message on LinkedIn ads, display ads, and sales emails creates pattern recognition. Message must be important if coming from multiple channels. Single channel outreach is easier to ignore
  • Reaching silent stakeholders - Sales team cannot easily email entire buying committee. Ads reach all employees including influencers you do not know about yet. Coverage across full organisation not just known contacts

Why advertising specifically for ABM

Traditional advertising targets broad audiences hoping some convert. Account-based advertising flips this, targeting only employees at your top 20-50 target accounts. Zero budget waste on irrelevant audiences. Every impression goes to someone you want to reach. ROI improves dramatically when targeting is this precise.

The surround-sound strategy: Account-based ads work best combined with direct sales outreach, not as replacement for it. Ads create ambient awareness. Sales creates direct engagement. Together they build momentum faster than either alone.

Best Practices

▼

Account List Size - Focus Not Spray

▼

The targeting trade-off

Account-based advertising budget is finite. Target too many accounts and nobody sees your ads enough times to build awareness. Target too few and you cannot generate enough pipeline to justify ad spend. Sweet spot is usually 20-50 accounts depending on budget and average account size.

How to size target list

  • Budget-driven approach - Calculate how many accounts you can reach with sufficient frequency. Assume £1,000-£2,000 per month per account for meaningful reach on LinkedIn. £20k monthly budget supports 10-20 accounts
  • Pipeline-driven approach - Work backwards from pipeline goals. If you need 10 new opportunities per quarter and ads drive opportunities at 20% rate, target 50 accounts
  • Sales capacity approach - Match ad targeting to sales capacity. If sales team can actively work 30 accounts, target those 30 with ads to support their efforts

List composition

Account TierCharacteristicsPercentage of List
Tier 1 - Active opportunities Current deals in pipeline. Ads accelerate and support active sales process 30-40% of list
Tier 2 - Priority targets High-fit accounts not yet engaged. Ads create awareness before sales outreach begins 40-50% of list
Tier 3 - Strategic accounts Long-term targets, key logos, or expansion opportunities. Ads build slow-burn awareness 10-20% of list
Concentration rule: If your target list exceeds 50 accounts and budget is constrained, split into cohorts. Run ads to Cohort A for 8 weeks, then switch to Cohort B. Better to reach 25 accounts effectively than 50 accounts inadequately.

Creative Strategy - Role-Specific Not Generic

▼

Why role-specific creative matters

Account-based advertising reaches entire buying committee from CFO to Operations Manager to Engineering Lead. Generic ads that try to appeal to all roles resonate with none. Role-specific creative acknowledges different priorities and increases relevance for each stakeholder.

Creative variations by role

Target RoleAd Message FocusExample Headline
C-suite Executives Business outcomes, competitive advantage, strategic impact "How leading enterprises are transforming customer engagement"
Finance / Budget Owners ROI, cost savings, efficiency gains, payback period "See how companies like yours achieve 3-to-1 ROI in first year"
Technical Buyers Architecture, security, integration, technical differentiators "Built for enterprise security and scale - see our architecture"
Operations / Implementation Ease of deployment, change management, support model "From signed to launched in 6 weeks - implementation guide"
End Users / Practitioners Daily workflow improvements, time savings, ease of use "Stop wasting hours on manual processes - see the new way"

Creative format best practices

  • Lead with value not features - Headlines should promise outcomes relevant to that role. Save feature details for landing page
  • Use social proof - Include customer logos or testimonials from similar companies. "Trusted by 500 enterprises" builds credibility
  • Clear call-to-action - Tell viewer exactly what to do next. "Download ROI guide," "Watch 3-min demo," "See customer stories"
  • Visual consistency - Maintain brand consistency across all creative variations so buying committee recognises ads as coming from same vendor
Testing approach: Start with 3-5 creative variations targeted at different roles. Run for 2-3 weeks then analyse which messages generate highest engagement. Double down on winning creative and pause underperformers. Continuously refresh creative every 4-6 weeks to avoid ad fatigue.

Coordination with Sales - Not Just Ads in Isolation

▼

Why coordination multiplies impact

Account-based ads alone rarely drive direct conversions. Prospects do not fill out forms from display ads. Ads create awareness that makes sales outreach more effective. Coordination between ads and sales turns parallel efforts into compounding force.

Coordination tactics

  • Timing sequence - Launch ads 2-3 weeks before sales outreach begins. Gives prospects time to see ads multiple times before receiving cold email. First email feels less cold when they have seen your brand
  • Message consistency - Align ad messaging with sales email messaging. If ads promote specific case study or value proposition, sales emails should reference same themes. Reinforces message through repetition
  • Account-level visibility - Share ad engagement data with sales team. Which accounts are seeing ads most? Which creative are they engaging with? Sales can reference this in outreach: "Given your interest in our approach to X..."
  • Retargeting site visitors - When prospects visit website from sales email, retarget them with ads reinforcing the message. Multi-touchpoint engagement increases conversion

Sales enablement talking points

Equip sales team to mention ads in outreach when appropriate. Not every email should reference ads, but when relevant, acknowledgement can build rapport.

Example: "You have probably been seeing us around LinkedIn lately. We are focusing on helping companies like Acme solve X. Would love to show you specifically how."

Feedback loop: Sales team should share which accounts are responding positively to outreach. Increase ad spend on responsive accounts to maintain momentum. Decrease or pause ad spend on accounts showing no interest after multiple touches. Reallocate budget dynamically based on sales feedback.

Measurement - Account Engagement Not Clicks

▼

Why traditional metrics mislead

Standard ad metrics like click-through rate and cost per click are designed for direct response campaigns. Account-based advertising is brand-building within specific accounts. Success is measured by account-level engagement and sales outcomes not individual ad clicks.

Account-based metrics that matter

MetricWhat It MeasuresTarget Benchmark
Account reach Percentage of target accounts seeing ads at least once 80%+ of target list
Frequency per account Average impressions per person at target accounts 8-12 impressions over 4 weeks
Account engagement rate Percentage of accounts with any engagement such as clicks or site visits 15-25% of accounts
Pipeline influence Opportunities created from advertised accounts versus non-advertised accounts 2-3x higher opportunity rate
Sales cycle impact Time from first touch to close for advertised accounts 10-20% faster sales cycles

Attribution challenges

Account-based ads work indirectly. Prospects see ads, become aware, then respond to sales outreach. Attribution models struggle with this because ads get no direct credit for conversions that happen via sales email. Use account-based reporting that shows lift in advertised accounts versus control group.

Measurement framework: Run ads to 30 target accounts while keeping 30 similar accounts as control group with no ads. Compare opportunity creation, pipeline velocity, and close rates between advertised and control groups. Difference demonstrates ad impact independent of attribution complexity.

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Targeting too many accounts - diluting budget

You target 200 accounts with limited budget. Nobody sees your ads enough times to build awareness. Budget spread so thin that impact is negligible.
Focus on 20-50 accounts maximum depending on budget. Better to reach 25 accounts with sufficient frequency than 200 accounts with one impression each. Concentration drives awareness.

2. Same creative for all roles - no personalisation

CFO, CTO, and Operations Manager all see identical generic ad. Nobody finds it relevant to their specific priorities. Engagement is low across the board.
Create 3-5 creative variations with role-specific messaging. Target ads based on job title or seniority. CFO sees ROI message, CTO sees technical message, Operations sees implementation message. Relevance drives engagement.

3. Not coordinating ads with sales outreach

Ads and sales operate in silos. Sales does not know which accounts are seeing ads. Ads do not align with sales messaging. Separate efforts that do not reinforce each other.
Launch ads 2-3 weeks before sales outreach. Share ad engagement data with sales team. Align ad and email messaging around consistent themes. Coordinate timing so ads warm up accounts before sales reaches out.

4. Measuring clicks instead of account engagement

You focus on click-through rate and cost per click. Miss the point that ads build awareness not drive direct conversions. Declare failure based on wrong metrics.
Measure account-level engagement such as reach, frequency, and site visits from target accounts. Compare opportunity rates between advertised and non-advertised accounts. Focus on sales impact not ad clicks.

5. No frequency caps - annoying prospects

You show same ad to same person 50 times in a week. Prospects get annoyed seeing your ad constantly. Brand perception suffers from overexposure.
Set frequency caps limiting impressions per person per week. Typical cap is 2-3 impressions per person per week. Enough to build awareness without becoming intrusive. Balance reach and frequency.

6. Running ads in isolation - not part of ABM programme

You run ads but no other ABM tactics. No personalised outreach, no custom content, no multi-threading. Ads alone without coordinated efforts fail to move deals.
Ads are one component of comprehensive ABM approach. Combine with personalised emails, custom landing pages, content hubs, and multi-stakeholder outreach. Integrated campaign drives results not single tactic.
