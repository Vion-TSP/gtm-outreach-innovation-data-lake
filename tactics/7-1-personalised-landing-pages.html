Tactic 7.1 - ABM Plays

Personalised Landing Pages

Create custom landing pages for high-value target accounts featuring their logo, industry-specific content, and tailored value propositions. Drive traffic via targeted ads or personalised emails to demonstrate relevance before first contact.

Effort: Medium-High Cost: Medium ICP: C-suite, VP-level Decision Makers
01

Why It Works

▼
The core insight: Decision makers at enterprise accounts receive hundreds of generic pitches weekly. A personalised landing page with their company logo and industry-specific examples signals that you've done your homework and understand their unique context, dramatically increasing engagement and response rates.

The psychology behind personalised pages

  • Pattern interruption - Seeing their own company logo on your page breaks the pattern of generic outreach. It forces them to pay attention because this is clearly custom-built for them
  • Reciprocity trigger - When someone invests effort in personalising something for you, you feel obligated to reciprocate by at least engaging with it. The visible effort creates psychological debt
  • Status validation - Being singled out for custom treatment signals importance. C-suite executives are accustomed to receiving VIP treatment; personalised pages tap into that expectation
  • Relevance proof - Industry-specific case studies and use cases immediately answer the question "Does this apply to me?" without requiring them to extrapolate from generic examples

Why landing pages specifically

Unlike cold emails that get deleted or LinkedIn messages that get ignored, a landing page is a destination you can drive traffic to through multiple channels. You can reference it in emails, link it from ads, share it in meetings, and track every interaction. It becomes a persistent asset that works for you long after the initial outreach.

The compounding effect: Each personalised landing page also serves as proof of capability. When prospects see you can build custom experiences quickly, it demonstrates the kind of tailored approach they can expect if they become a customer.

04

Best Practices

▼

Account Selection - Focus on Top 20

▼

Why limit to top 20 accounts

Personalised landing pages require significant investment of time and research. Building 100 mediocre personalised pages will underperform building 20 genuinely customised experiences. Quality over quantity is essential for this tactic to generate ROI.

Account prioritisation criteria

  • Deal size potential - Focus on accounts where average deal value justifies the 2-3 hour investment per page. If your ACV is under £10k, this tactic may not be worth it
  • Trigger events - Recent funding rounds, leadership changes, expansion announcements, or tech stack changes signal buying intent. Target accounts showing active change
  • Warm connections - Do you have a mutual connection, existing relationship with someone at the company, or warm intro path? Personalised pages work best when combined with relationship leverage
  • Strategic importance - Some accounts are valuable beyond immediate revenue such as logo value, industry influence, or potential for large expansion. Include strategic accounts even if initial deal is smaller

Account research checklist

Research AreaWhere to Find ItWhat to Look For
Company priorities LinkedIn posts from executives, earnings calls, annual reports Strategic initiatives, growth targets, transformation programmes mentioned repeatedly
Pain points Job postings, Glassdoor reviews, industry reports Roles they are hiring for signal gaps. Common complaints in reviews reveal process issues
Tech stack BuiltWith, job postings mentioning tools, LinkedIn skill endorsements Technologies they currently use and integrations you can highlight. Missing tools you complement
Competitive landscape G2, Capterra, industry analyst reports What solutions they might already be evaluating. Competitive positioning opportunities
Time-saving tip: Build research profiles in a spreadsheet for all top 20 accounts before building any pages. This lets you identify patterns across accounts and create reusable content sections for companies in similar industries or with similar challenges.

Content Personalisation - Beyond Logo Placement

▼

Levels of personalisation depth

Simply dropping a company logo on a generic page is not true personalisation. Effective personalised landing pages require multiple layers of customisation that demonstrate genuine understanding of the target account context.

Personalisation layers to include

  • Custom headline - Address their specific challenge. "James, here's how Acme can reduce customer churn by 30%" not "Reduce customer churn with our platform"
  • Industry-specific examples - Show case studies and metrics from their exact industry. Financial services companies care about different outcomes than healthcare companies
  • Relevant integrations - Highlight integrations with tools you know they use. If they use Salesforce and HubSpot, show those logos prominently
  • Localised data - Include statistics and benchmarks specific to their geography or market segment. UK data for UK companies, enterprise benchmarks for enterprise accounts
  • Personalised CTA - "See how companies like Acme use our platform" not generic "Book a demo" button

Content sections that work

SectionPurposePersonalisation Element
Hero with target logo Immediate pattern interrupt and attention capture Their company logo plus personalised headline mentioning their specific challenge
Industry challenges overview Demonstrate understanding of their context 2-3 pain points specific to their industry with supporting data from industry reports
Similar customer stories Provide social proof and concrete examples Case studies from companies in same industry, similar size, or same geography
Tailored solution overview Connect your offering to their specific needs Feature highlights mapped directly to pain points mentioned earlier. Show integrations with their tech stack
ROI calculator or assessment Quantify potential value in their context Pre-populated with estimates based on their company size and industry benchmarks
Authenticity check: Before launching a personalised page, ask yourself: "If I removed the company logo, would it still be obvious this page was built for this specific account?" If the answer is no, add more contextual personalisation.

Traffic Generation - Email and Targeted Ads

▼

Multi-channel approach

Building a brilliant personalised landing page delivers zero value if nobody visits it. Use a coordinated multi-channel approach to drive your target stakeholders to the page, with email and account-based advertising as primary channels.

Personalised email approach

  • Direct mention in subject line - "Sarah - built this for Acme" or "Acme-specific analysis inside" to drive opens
  • Explain the effort - In email body, explicitly mention you built something custom: "I put together a personalised overview of how companies like Acme are solving X. Takes 3 minutes to review."
  • Teaser content - Include one insight from the page in the email itself to demonstrate value before they click
  • Clear CTA - Single button with copy like "View Your Custom Analysis" not generic "Click here"

Account-based advertising approach

PlatformTargeting MethodBest Use Case
LinkedIn Campaign Manager Target by company name plus job titles Reaching multiple stakeholders at target account simultaneously. Can run ads to 5-10 decision makers at same company driving them to personalised page
RollWorks or Demandbase IP-based targeting of company office locations Display ads that appear across the web when target account employees browse. Reinforces message across multiple touchpoints
Google Search Ads Branded keywords for target company When someone at Acme searches "Acme + your category," show ad driving to their personalised page. Captures active research behaviour

Timing and sequencing

Launch personalised email and ads simultaneously for maximum impact. Email reaches named contacts while ads create ambient awareness across broader buying committee. Retarget page visitors with different messaging acknowledging they have seen the custom content.

Budget guidance: For LinkedIn ads targeting a single account, budget £500-£1000 per month to reach 10-20 decision makers with sufficient frequency. Test email-only approach first if ad budget is constrained.

Engagement Tracking - Know Who Visits

▼

What to track

Personalised landing pages generate valuable intent signals when tracked properly. Go beyond basic page views to understand depth of engagement and follow up intelligently based on visitor behaviour.

Essential tracking metrics

  • Individual visitor identification - Use tools like Clearbit Reveal, 6sense, or LinkedIn Insight Tag to identify which individuals from target account visited the page
  • Time on page - 30 seconds suggests they scanned it. 3 minutes suggests they read carefully. Prioritise follow-up with longer engagement
  • Section scrolling - Did they scroll to case studies? Did they reach the ROI calculator? Different sections indicate different interests
  • Return visits - Someone who visits 3 times over a week is signalling strong interest even if they have not filled out a form
  • Content downloads or form fills - Explicit conversion actions demand immediate follow-up

Follow-up based on engagement level

Engagement SignalFollow-Up ActionTiming
Page view under 30 seconds Send follow-up email with different angle or additional resource Wait 3-5 days
Page view 1-3 minutes, single visit Email or LinkedIn message referencing they viewed it: "Saw you checked out the analysis. Questions on anything specific?" Within 24 hours
Multiple visits or 3+ minutes engaged Direct phone call or personalised video message: "You have spent time with the content. Let's talk about how this applies to Acme." Within 12 hours
Form fill or content download Immediate call or meeting request. They have raised their hand. Strike while interest is high Within 2 hours
Privacy consideration: Be thoughtful about how explicitly you reveal tracking. "I noticed you spent some time with the page" is less creepy than "I saw you visited 3 times for a total of 7 minutes and 23 seconds." Signal awareness without being invasive.
05

Common Mistakes

▼

1. Personalisation only skin-deep - just logo swap

Adding target company logo to otherwise generic page feels lazy and inauthentic. Prospects immediately recognise superficial effort and disengage.
Personalise headline, case studies, industry data, pain points, and integrations. Every section should reflect understanding of their specific context. Aim for 5+ personalised elements beyond the logo.

2. Creating pages for too many accounts

Trying to build 50-100 personalised pages results in templated, shallow personalisation that does not move the needle. Quality degrades as volume increases.
Focus on top 20 highest-value target accounts. Invest 2-3 hours per page to make each genuinely custom. Better to have 20 excellent pages than 100 mediocre ones.

3. No clear call-to-action

Visitor reads the page, finds it interesting, but has no clear next step. They close the tab and move on. Personalisation effort wasted.
Include prominent, specific CTA such as "Book 15 min with Sarah" or "Get your custom ROI analysis." Make next step frictionless and relevant to the content they just consumed.

4. Slow page load times

Heavy images, complex animations, or bloated code cause page to load slowly. Impatient executives close the tab before it finishes loading.
Optimise all images before uploading. Test page load speed on mobile and desktop. Aim for under 2 second load time. Use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights to identify issues.

5. Not tracking who visits

You send people to personalised page but have no idea if they visited, who visited, or what they engaged with. Flying blind on follow-up.
Implement visitor identification tools such as Clearbit Reveal or LinkedIn Insight Tag. Set up engagement alerts so you know immediately when target stakeholders visit. Track scroll depth and time on page.

6. Building pages but not driving traffic

You create beautiful personalised landing pages then passively hope people find them. Without active traffic generation, even the best page sits unused.
Always pair page creation with multi-channel promotion plan. Send personalised emails, run LinkedIn ads targeted at the account, mention in sales calls. Every page needs a distribution strategy.
